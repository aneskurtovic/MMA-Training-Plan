# Week 4 — DELOAD

**Focus:** Recovery and supercompensation. Volume cut 40-50%, intensity at 70-75% 1RM, NO hard sparring. Every session this week serves recovery — if something feels heavy or grinding, back off immediately. The goal is to arrive at next week fresher and stronger than you started this block.

**Athlete:** 87-90 kg, 185 cm | Squat 150 kg | Deadlift 180 kg | Bench 115 kg | OHP 75 kg

## Intensity Key

| Marker | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 🟢 | Easy — recovery pace, low CNS demand |
| 🟡 | Moderate — working hard but controlled |

> There are ZERO 🔴 sessions this week. If you find yourself pushing into 🔴 territory, you are doing it wrong.

## Week 4 Recovery Checklist

Use this daily. Check off each item before bed.

### Daily Non-Negotiables

- [ ] **Sleep:** 8-9 hours minimum (9 on Wednesday and Sunday)
- [ ] **Hydration:** 3-4 litres of water. Urine should be clear/light yellow.
- [ ] **Protein:** 2g/kg bodyweight minimum (~180g/day). Spread across 4-5 meals.
- [ ] **Foam Rolling:** 10 min minimum. Full body, extra time on sore spots.
- [ ] **Stretching/Mobility:** 5-10 min dedicated stretching (separate from training cool-downs)

### Daily Bonus Recovery Actions

- [ ] **Cold/Contrast Exposure:** Contrast shower (2 min hot / 30 sec cold x 3) or cold water immersion
- [ ] **Breathing Work:** 3-5 min box breathing or nasal-only breathing practice
- [ ] **Screen Curfew:** No screens 30 min before bed
- [ ] **Magnesium:** Pre-bed magnesium if part of your supplement protocol
- [ ] **Walk:** 15-20 min easy walk outdoors (sunlight exposure for circadian rhythm)

### Weekly Targets

- [ ] Zero 🔴 intensity sessions (if any session felt hard, you pushed too much)
- [ ] All HR-based sessions stayed below prescribed caps (145 BPM cardiac output, 140 BPM Thursday conditioning, 130 BPM Saturday easy cardio)
- [ ] Body weight stable or slightly up (you should NOT be cutting during deload)
- [ ] No new aches, pains, or injuries — existing soreness should be reducing day by day
- [ ] Benchmark tests completed Saturday and logged
- [ ] Results compared to Week 0 baselines from conditioning-tests.md
- [ ] Training log updated with all weights, reps, and RPE data for the week
- [ ] Mental state: feeling eager to train hard again (not dreading next week)

### Signs the Deload Worked

- Resting heart rate has dropped compared to end of Week 3
- Grip strength and bar speed feel noticeably better by Friday
- Mood and motivation are up
- Sleep quality has improved
- Minor aches from Week 3 have resolved
- Benchmark test results match or exceed Week 0 baselines

### Signs You Need More Recovery

- Still feeling flat or fatigued by Thursday
- Resting heart rate hasn't come down
- Sleep quality is still poor
- Lingering joint pain or muscle soreness
- No motivation to train

> If you see multiple warning signs above, consider extending the deload by 2-3 days before starting the next block. Recovery is not weakness — it is where adaptation happens.
