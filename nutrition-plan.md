# Daily Nutrition & Meal Plan — Weight Cut Camp

**Fighter Profile:** 89 kg → 77 kg | **Fight Date:** May 16, 2026 | **Camp:** 9 Weeks (March 16 – May 16)

> This plan provides specific daily meal options for each phase of the weight cut. Every meal slot has 2–3 options for variety. Protein is kept at 2.3 g/kg throughout to preserve muscle during the caloric deficit. Carbohydrates are cycled by training demand. Fat is kept at the minimum needed for hormonal health.

---

## Quick Reference — Phase Calorie & Macro Targets

| Phase | Weeks | Body Weight (approx.) | Training Day | Rest Day | Protein | Notes |
|-------|-------|-----------------------|-------------|----------|---------|-------|
| **Phase 1** | 1–3 | 89→85 kg | ~2,600 kcal | ~2,100 kcal | ~200 g (2.3 g/kg × 87) | Base cut, establish deficit |
| **Phase 2** | 4–6 | 85→81 kg | ~2,400 kcal | ~1,900 kcal | ~191 g (2.3 g/kg × 83) | Deeper deficit |
| **Phase 3** | 7–8 | 81→79 kg | ~2,400 kcal | ~2,000 kcal | ~184 g (2.3 g/kg × 80) | Protect performance |
| **Phase 4** | 9 | 79→77 kg | Minimal/strategic | — | Reduced | Water/sodium cut |

---

## General Guidelines

- **Meal prep:** Cook protein in bulk (chicken breast, lean beef, eggs) on Sundays and Wednesdays.
- **Weigh food raw** (meat, rice, oats) for accuracy.
- **Hydrate:** Water intake targets are listed per phase. Drink consistently throughout the day — do not chug before meals.
- **Supplements (all phases):** Multivitamin, 5 g creatine (drop in Phase 4), fish oil 2 g, magnesium 400 mg before bed.
- **Sodium:** Keep moderate in Phases 1–3 (~2,000–2,500 mg/day). Manipulated in Phase 4.
- **Caffeine:** Black coffee or green tea only — no sugar, no milk during cut (milk adds unnecessary calories).

### Budget-Friendly Options

Every meal table includes a **Budget** option using cheaper ingredients (eggs, canned tuna/sardines/mackerel, chicken thighs, oats, white rice, frozen vegetables, milk, potatoes, bananas) that hit similar macro targets. If money is tight, choose the Budget option for every meal.

### Key vs Optional Meals

On a tight budget, prioritize the **key meals** and treat the snacks as optional — fold their calories into the key meals if needed.

| Meal | Priority | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| **Breakfast** | KEY | Breaks the overnight fast, fuels the morning |
| **Mid-Morning Snack** | Optional | Can skip if you eat a larger breakfast |
| **Lunch / Pre-Training** | KEY | Fuels your training session directly |
| **Pre-Training Snack** | Optional | A banana is enough; skip if lunch was close to training |
| **Post-Training** | KEY | Critical recovery window — do not skip |
| **Dinner** | KEY | Main recovery meal of the day |
| **Pre-Bed** | Optional | Can skip if dinner was large and protein-rich enough |
