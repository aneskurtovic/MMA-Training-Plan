## Fight Week Overview

This is not a training week. This is a **preparation and execution** week. The work is done. Your only jobs now are:

1. Make weight safely
2. Stay mentally sharp
3. Arrive at the fight healthy, hydrated, and confident

The 2 kg water cut from 79 kg to 77 kg is a **mild cut** and should be manageable with proper water loading and sodium manipulation. Follow the protocols below exactly as planned with your coach.

---

## Water Loading Protocol

Begin water loading on Sunday. The goal is to upregulate your body's water excretion mechanisms by drinking large volumes, then sharply reducing intake so the body continues flushing water even when intake drops.

| Day | Date | Water Intake | Notes |
|-----|------|-------------|-------|
| Sunday | May 11 | 8 L | Begin water loading. Spread across waking hours. |
| Monday | May 12 | 8 L | Continue loading. Urine should be clear and frequent. |
| Tuesday | May 13 | 6 L | Begin tapering water. Body still excreting at high rate. |
| Wednesday | May 14 | 4 L | Further reduction. Noticeable water loss begins. |
| Thursday | May 15 | 2 L | Minimal water. Sip throughout day. Stop by evening. |
| Friday | May 16 | Sips only | Small sips until weigh-in. Full rehydration after. |

**Key Rules:**
- Spread water intake evenly across the day (do not chug large volumes at once)
- Distilled water is preferred during loading days (lower mineral content)
- Track all fluid intake — use a measured bottle
- Weigh yourself morning and evening to track progress
- If weight is not trending on schedule, consult coach before adjusting

## Sodium Manipulation Protocol

Sodium manipulation works alongside water loading. High sodium early causes water retention; dropping sodium causes the body to flush stored water.

| Day | Date | Sodium Intake | Food Notes |
|-----|------|--------------|------------|
| Sunday | May 11 | Normal (~3,000-4,000 mg) | Eat normally, add salt to food |
| Monday | May 12 | Normal (~3,000-4,000 mg) | Continue normal salting |
| Tuesday | May 13 | Reduced (~1,500-2,000 mg) | Begin reducing — avoid processed food, no added salt |
| Wednesday | May 14 | Low (~500-1,000 mg) | Plain foods only — rice, chicken, vegetables, no seasoning |
| Thursday | May 15 | Minimal (~200-500 mg) | Very plain food, small portions, no added salt |
| Friday | May 16 | None until post-weigh-in | No food until after weigh-in |

**Carbohydrate Notes:**
- Sunday-Tuesday: Normal carb intake
- Wednesday: Reduce carbs to ~100g (reduces glycogen-bound water)
- Thursday: Reduce carbs to ~50g
- Friday: No food until post-weigh-in

## Rehydration Protocol (Post-Weigh-In)

The goal is to rehydrate from ~77 kg back toward ~79-80 kg before the fight. You have approximately 8-10 hours to rehydrate.

| Timeframe | Fluid | Food | Target |
|-----------|-------|------|--------|
| 0-30 min post-weigh-in | 500 mL ORS (oral rehydration solution) | Banana, white bread + honey | Begin absorption |
| 30-90 min | 500 mL ORS, sip steadily | Pre-fight Meal 1 (see below) | |
| 90 min - 3 hours | 500 mL water/electrolyte | Pre-fight Meal 2 (see below) | ~1.5 L consumed |
| 3-6 hours | Sip water/electrolytes as desired | Light snacks as desired | ~2-2.5 L consumed |
| 6-8 hours | Small sips only | Final small snack (2 hrs before fight) | ~3 L total consumed |

**Rehydration Rules:**
- **NEVER chug water.** Sip steadily. Chugging causes bloating, nausea, and poor absorption.
- Use an oral rehydration solution (ORS) or add electrolyte tabs to water (Pedialyte, LMNT, or similar)
- Do NOT drink plain water only — you need sodium and potassium to retain fluid
- Stop drinking large volumes 2 hours before fight time
- Target total fluid intake: 2.5-3.5 L between weigh-in and fight
- Monitor urine color — aim for pale yellow, not clear (clear means you're flushing, not retaining)

## Warning Signs During the Water Cut

Monitor for these warning signs throughout the week. **If any STOP signs appear, halt the cut immediately and seek medical advice.**

### Normal / Expected
| Symptom | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Mild thirst | Expected from Wednesday onward |
| Slightly reduced energy | Normal, conserve energy |
| Darker urine | Expected as water intake drops |
| Mild irritability | Normal — manage with breathing exercises |
| Slight headache (brief) | Can occur, usually resolves |
| Dry mouth | Expected Thursday/Friday |

### Caution — Slow Down
| Symptom | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Persistent headache | Small sips of electrolyte solution, reassess in 30 min |
| Mild dizziness when standing | Sit down, sip electrolytes, move slowly |
| Muscle cramps | Electrolytes needed — small sips of ORS |
| Extreme irritability / mood swings | Rest, breathe, reduce any remaining activity |
| Poor sleep quality | Accept it, stay in bed, rest even if not sleeping |

### STOP — Seek Help Immediately
| Symptom | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Severe dizziness or fainting | **STOP CUT.** Drink electrolytes. Call coach/doctor. |
| Rapid or irregular heartbeat | **STOP CUT.** Seek medical attention immediately. |
| Confusion or disorientation | **STOP CUT.** This is dangerous. Rehydrate now. |
| Inability to stand | **STOP CUT.** Medical emergency possible. |
| Chest pain | **STOP CUT. CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES.** |
| Vomiting (unable to keep fluids down) | **STOP CUT.** Seek medical help — dehydration will worsen. |
| Seizures or muscle spasms (severe) | **STOP CUT. CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES.** |

> **Remember:** No fight is worth your health. A 2 kg water cut is mild and should be straightforward. If it becomes anything more than mildly uncomfortable, something is wrong. Speak up.
