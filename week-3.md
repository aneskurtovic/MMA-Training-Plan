# Week 3 — PEAK VOLUME / INTENSITY

**Mesocycle Role:** This is the hardest week of the block. Volume and intensity are at their highest before the Week 4 deload. RPE targets are 8-9 across the board.

**Tempo Shift:** The triphasic progression advances this week. Eccentric control remains (3 seconds down), but the concentric is now **EXPLOSIVE (X)**. Full tempo notation: **3-1-X-0** (3s eccentric, 1s pause, explosive concentric, no pause at top).

**Fatigue Management:**
- Expect significant accumulated fatigue by Thursday. Sleep 8-9 hours minimum, especially Tue-Wed nights.
- Nutrition must be on point — aim for 3,800-4,000 kcal on double-session days. Prioritize carbs around training.
- Thursday is the hardest day of the entire block (HIIT AM + full MMA sparring PM). Pre-load with extra carbs Wednesday evening.
- If anything feels genuinely "off" (sharp joint pain, dizziness, unusual HR), scale back. Week 4 deload is coming — push hard but don't break.

> **Peak conditioning session of the block. 5 rounds simulating fight-length efforts.**

**Warm-Up (5 min):** Jump rope + dynamic stretches.

**Fight Simulation Circuit — 5 rounds x 5 min, 60s rest between rounds**

Each round cycles through these stations (45s work / 15s transition):

| Station | Exercise | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Heavy Bag — Combo Flurries | Max output for 45s |
| 2 | Sprawl to Shot | Continuous — fight pace |
| 3 | Assault Bike Sprint | All-out |
| 4 | Ground-and-Pound on Bag | Mount or guard position |
| 5 | Clinch Knees on Bag | Drive knees, maintain control |

- 60s rest between rounds (1:1 work-to-rest for 5-min rounds). Walk and breathe.
- This is HARD. The goal is to maintain output quality across all 5 rounds. When round 4-5 feel terrible, keep technique clean.

**Cooldown (5 min):** Walk, nasal breathing, light stretching.
