# MMA Nutrition Framework

**Athlete Profile:** 87-90 kg (88 kg reference weight) | Amateur MMA Fighter

---

## 1. Macro Calculations

**Core Principle:** Protein stays constant every day. Carbs cycle based on training demand. Fat stays relatively stable.

### Per-Kilogram Targets (88 kg)

| Nutrient | g/kg     | Grams | Notes                        |
|----------|----------|-------|------------------------------|
| Protein  | 2.1      | 185 g | Constant across all days     |
| Carbs    | Varies   | —     | Cycled by training volume    |
| Fat      | ~1.0     | 85-88 g | Minimum for hormonal health |

### Daily Totals by Day Type

| Day Type                  | Protein | Carbs | Fat  | kcal    | Carb g/kg |
|---------------------------|---------|-------|------|---------|-----------|
| **Training Day**          | 185 g   | 400 g | 85 g | ~3,100  | 4.5       |
| **High-Volume Double Day**| 185 g   | 440 g | 88 g | ~3,300* | 5.0       |
| **Rest Day**              | 185 g   | 264 g | 85 g | ~2,560  | 3.0       |

*\*Double session days: base intake is ~3,300 kcal + intra-workout carbs bring effective total to ~3,600 kcal.*

### The Math

```
Protein:  88 kg × 2.1 g/kg = 185 g → 185 × 4 =  740 kcal
Fat:      88 kg × 1.0 g/kg =  85 g →  85 × 9 =  765 kcal

Training day carbs:    88 × 4.5 = 400 g → 400 × 4 = 1,600 kcal → Total: 3,105 kcal
Double day carbs:      88 × 5.0 = 440 g → 440 × 4 = 1,760 kcal → Total: 3,265 kcal + intra
Rest day carbs:        88 × 3.0 = 264 g → 264 × 4 = 1,056 kcal → Total: 2,561 kcal
```

## 2. Meal Timing

### Pre-Training (2-3 hours before)

| Nutrient | Target     | Grams (88 kg) | Goal                              |
|----------|------------|----------------|------------------------------------|
| Carbs    | 1-2 g/kg   | 88-176 g       | Top off glycogen                   |
| Protein  | 0.3-0.4 g/kg | 26-35 g      | Start muscle protein synthesis     |
| Fat      | Low        | < 15 g         | Speed gastric emptying             |

### Pre-Training Snack (30-60 min before)

- **30-50 g fast-digesting carbs** — banana, white rice cake with honey, sports drink
- Keep fat and fiber minimal to avoid GI distress during training

### Intra-Workout (double sessions or any session > 90 min)

- **30-60 g carbs per hour** — sports drink, gummy bears, highly branched cyclic dextrin
- **Electrolytes** — 500-700 mg sodium per liter of fluid
- Sip continuously; do not chug between rounds

### Post-Training (within 1-2 hours)

| Nutrient | Target       | Grams (88 kg) | Goal                          |
|----------|--------------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| Protein  | 0.3-0.5 g/kg | 26-44 g        | Kickstart recovery/MPS        |
| Carbs    | 1-1.5 g/kg   | 88-132 g       | Replenish glycogen             |

## 4. Hydration Protocol

### Daily Baseline

| Metric | Calculation | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Body weight method | 88 kg × 35-45 ml/kg | **3.1 - 4.0 L per day** |

Aim for the higher end on training days and in warm conditions.

### Around Training

| Window | Protocol |
|--------|----------|
| **Pre-training** (2 hours before) | 500 ml water, steadily sipped |
| **During training** | 600-1,200 ml per hour with electrolytes |
| **Post-training** | Replace 150% of fluid lost (weigh before/after if possible) |

### Electrolyte Targets During Training

- **Sodium:** 500-700 mg per liter of fluid
- **Potassium:** 150-200 mg per liter
- Add a pinch of salt to water or use a commercial electrolyte mix

### Monitoring

- **Urine color:** Aim for pale straw yellow. Clear = overhydrated. Dark yellow/amber = dehydrated.
- Check first thing in the morning — this is your best baseline indicator.
- If weigh-in shows > 2% body weight loss post-session, increase fluid intake next session.

## 5. Supplement Stack

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Evidence | Key Benefit | Notes |
|------------|------|--------|----------|-------------|-------|
| **Creatine Monohydrate** | 5 g/day | Any time (consistency matters) | A-tier | Strength, power output, recovery | No loading phase needed. Take daily including rest days. Mix in water or shake. |
| **Caffeine** | 3-6 mg/kg (264-528 mg) | 45-60 min pre-training | A-tier | Alertness, power, endurance | **Avoid after 2:00 PM** to protect sleep. Start at lower dose, assess tolerance. Cycle off 1 week/month if desired. |
| **Whey Protein** | 30-40 g per serving | Post-training or any meal gap | A-tier | Convenient complete protein | Use to hit daily 185 g target. Not a replacement for whole food protein. |
| **Electrolytes** | 500-700 mg sodium/L | During training > 60 min | A-tier | Prevent cramping, maintain output | Commercial mix or DIY (1/4 tsp salt + water + squeeze of lemon). |
| **Vitamin D3** | 3,000-5,000 IU/day | With a fat-containing meal | A-tier | Immune function, bone health, recovery | Get blood levels tested; target 40-60 ng/ml. Higher dose in winter / low sun. |
| **Magnesium Glycinate** | 400-500 mg/day | Split AM/PM; prioritize before bed | B-tier | Sleep quality, muscle relaxation, recovery | Glycinate form is best absorbed and least likely to cause GI issues. |
| **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** | 2-3 g combined EPA+DHA/day | With meals (split across 2 meals) | A-tier | Anti-inflammatory, joint health, brain function | Look for high-concentration fish oil. Keep refrigerated. |
| **Beta-Alanine** | 3-6 g/day | Split into 2-3 doses (reduces tingling) | B-tier | Buffers lactic acid in 1-4 min efforts | Optional. Most useful for high-rep grappling rounds. Tingling (paresthesia) is harmless. |

## 7. Quick-Reference Daily Checklists

### Training Day

- [ ] Hit protein target: 185 g across all meals
- [ ] Hit carb target: ~400 g (scale up to 440 g on double days)
- [ ] Pre-training meal 2-3 hours before: carbs + protein, low fat
- [ ] Pre-training snack 30-60 min before: banana or fast carbs
- [ ] Intra-workout drink if session > 90 min: carbs + electrolytes
- [ ] Post-training meal within 1-2 hours: protein + carbs
- [ ] Water intake: 3.5-4.0 L total (more in heat)
- [ ] 500 ml water in 2 hours before training
- [ ] 600-1,200 ml/hour during training with electrolytes
- [ ] Creatine: 5 g taken
- [ ] Caffeine: taken 45-60 min pre-training, not after 2 PM
- [ ] Omega-3 with a meal
- [ ] Vitamin D with a fat-containing meal
- [ ] Magnesium before bed
- [ ] Pre-bed protein source (casein-rich: cottage cheese, Greek yogurt)

### Rest Day

- [ ] Hit protein target: 185 g across all meals
- [ ] Hit carb target: ~264 g (lower than training days)
- [ ] Keep fat at ~85 g (slightly higher-fat protein sources are fine)
- [ ] Eat 4-5 meals spaced 3-4 hours apart
- [ ] Water intake: 3.0-3.5 L minimum
- [ ] Creatine: 5 g taken (do not skip on rest days)
- [ ] No caffeine after 2 PM (or skip entirely for tolerance reset)
- [ ] Omega-3 with a meal
- [ ] Vitamin D with a fat-containing meal
- [ ] Magnesium before bed
- [ ] Pre-bed protein source
- [ ] Prioritize whole foods over shakes today
