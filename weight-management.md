# Weight Management

**Fight Date:** May 16, 2026 | **Weight Class:** 77 kg | **Camp Duration:** 9 weeks

---

## 1. Weight Cut Overview

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Starting weight | 89 kg |
| Target weigh-in weight | 77 kg |
| Total cut | 12 kg |
| Chronic dieting (Weeks 1-8) | ~9 kg (fat loss + glycogen depletion) |
| Acute water cut (Week 9) | ~3 kg |
| Camp duration | 9 weeks |

**Strategy:** Lose the majority of weight through sustained caloric deficit over 8 weeks, preserving as much muscle and performance as possible. Reserve the final 3 kg for a water/sodium manipulation protocol in fight week. This keeps the acute water cut within safe limits (~3.8% of body weight at 79 kg).

> **WARNING:** A 12 kg total cut is aggressive. This requires strict dietary compliance, consistent monitoring, and ideally supervision from a sports nutritionist experienced with combat athletes. If weight loss stalls or performance degrades significantly, reassess the target weight class.

## 2. Per-Phase Caloric Targets

| Phase | Weeks | Caloric Deficit | Training Day kcal | Rest Day kcal | Target Weight (end of phase) | Notes |
|-------|-------|----------------|-------------------|---------------|------------------------------|-------|
| **Phase 1 — Base Cut** | 1-3 | ~500 kcal/day | ~2,600 | ~2,100 | 85 kg | Establish deficit, maintain training intensity. ~1.3 kg/week loss. |
| **Phase 2 — Accelerated Cut** | 4-6 | ~600 kcal/day | ~2,400 | ~1,900 | 81 kg | Slightly deeper deficit. Increase protein to offset muscle loss. ~1.3 kg/week loss. |
| **Phase 3 — Final Trim** | 7-8 | ~500 kcal/day | ~2,400 | ~2,000 | 79 kg | Reduce deficit slightly to protect performance before fight. Begin water loading prep at end of Week 8. ~1 kg/week loss. |
| **Phase 4 — Fight Week** | 9 | Water/sodium manipulation | Minimal structured meals | — | 77 kg | Glycogen depletion + water cut for final ~3 kg. Food intake is minimal and strategic. |

**Key principles:**
- Protein stays at 2.3 g/kg throughout (higher than maintenance to preserve lean mass during deficit)
- Deficit is created primarily by reducing carbohydrates and fats — never by cutting protein
- Training day calories are always higher than rest day calories (carb cycling)

## 4. Macronutrient Framework

### Per-Kilogram Targets

| Nutrient | g/kg | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| **Protein** | 2.3 | Higher than maintenance (was 2.1) to preserve muscle during deficit. ~185 g constant. |
| **Carbs** | Varies by day type | Cycled by training demand — see table below |
| **Fat** | 0.8-1.0 | Minimum for hormonal health. ~65-80 g depending on phase. |

### Daily Totals by Day Type (Phase 1-2 Example at ~83 kg)

| Day Type | Protein | Carbs | Fat | kcal | Carb g/kg |
|----------|---------|-------|-----|------|-----------|
| **Training Day** | 185 g | 280-300 g | 70 g | ~2,400-2,600 | 3.4-3.6 |
| **Double Session Day** | 185 g | 320 g | 75 g | ~2,700 | 3.9 |
| **Rest Day** | 185 g | 170-200 g | 65 g | ~1,900-2,100 | 2.0-2.4 |

**Adjustments as weight drops:**
- Protein stays at 185 g constant (this becomes a higher g/kg as weight decreases — protective)
- Carbs are the primary lever — reduce by 20-30 g per phase as needed
- Fat should not drop below 0.8 g/kg (~65 g at 80 kg) to maintain testosterone and recovery

## 5. Meal Timing

### Pre-Training (2-3 hours before)

| Nutrient | Target | Grams | Goal |
|----------|--------|-------|------|
| Carbs | 1-1.5 g/kg | 80-120 g | Top off glycogen without excess calories |
| Protein | 0.3-0.4 g/kg | 25-35 g | Start muscle protein synthesis |
| Fat | Low | < 10 g | Speed gastric emptying |

### Pre-Training Snack (30-60 min before)

- **30-40 g fast-digesting carbs** — banana, white rice cake with honey, sports drink
- Keep fat and fiber minimal to avoid GI distress during training

### Intra-Workout (double sessions or any session > 90 min)

- **30-60 g carbs per hour** — sports drink, gummy bears, highly branched cyclic dextrin
- **Electrolytes** — 500-700 mg sodium per liter of fluid
- Sip continuously; do not chug between rounds
- During a cut, intra-workout carbs are even more critical to protect training quality

### Post-Training (within 1-2 hours)

| Nutrient | Target | Grams | Goal |
|----------|--------|-------|------|
| Protein | 0.3-0.5 g/kg | 25-40 g | Kickstart recovery/MPS |
| Carbs | 1-1.2 g/kg | 80-100 g | Replenish glycogen |

## 7. Hydration Protocol

### Daily Baseline (Non-Fight-Week)

| Metric | Calculation | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Body weight method | Current weight x 35-45 ml/kg | **3.0-3.8 L per day** |

Aim for the higher end on training days and in warm conditions.

### Around Training

| Window | Protocol |
|--------|----------|
| **Pre-training** (2 hours before) | 500 ml water, steadily sipped |
| **During training** | 600-1,200 ml per hour with electrolytes |
| **Post-training** | Replace 150% of fluid lost (weigh before/after if possible) |

### Electrolyte Targets During Training

- **Sodium:** 500-700 mg per liter of fluid
- **Potassium:** 150-200 mg per liter
- Add a pinch of salt to water or use a commercial electrolyte mix

### Monitoring

- **Urine color:** Aim for pale straw yellow. Clear = overhydrated. Dark yellow/amber = dehydrated.
- Check first thing in the morning — this is your best baseline indicator.
- If weigh-in shows > 2% body weight loss post-session, increase fluid intake next session.

## 8. Water Loading Protocol (Fight Week — Week 9)

> **WARNING: Water manipulation is DANGEROUS if done incorrectly. Work with an experienced coach or nutritionist who has managed weight cuts before. The protocol below is an outline only — individual responses vary significantly.**

**Pre-conditions:** You must be at or below 79 kg entering fight week. If you are above 80 kg at the start of Week 9, reconsider whether the water cut is safe — you may need to cut more than 3 kg acutely, which increases risk.

| Days Out from Weigh-In | Water Intake | Sodium | Carbs | Food | Notes |
|-------------------------|-------------|--------|-------|------|-------|
| **7 days (Saturday)** | 8 L/day | Normal (~3,000 mg) | Normal | Normal meals | Begin water loading — train kidneys to excrete at high rate |
| **6 days (Sunday)** | 8 L/day | Normal | Normal | Normal meals | Continue loading. Spread intake across waking hours. |
| **5 days (Monday)** | 8 L/day | Normal | Normal | Normal meals | Continue loading. Monitor urine — should be very clear. |
| **4 days (Tuesday)** | 6 L/day | Reduce by 50% (~1,500 mg) | Normal | Normal meals | Begin tapering. Body still excreting at high rate. |
| **3 days (Wednesday)** | 4 L/day | Reduce to minimal (~500 mg) | Begin reducing (~150 g) | Smaller meals, lean protein + low-fiber carbs | Taper continues. Avoid high-sodium foods. |
| **2 days (Thursday)** | 2 L/day | Minimal (~300 mg) | Low (~75 g) | Small meals: chicken, rice, banana | Body still excreting at high rate but intake is low. Weight drops noticeably. |
| **1 day (Friday — 24h before weigh-in)** | Sips only (~500 ml max) | Minimal | Very low (~50 g) | Light: chicken breast, small portion rice | Restrict to small sips as needed. Hot bath (20 min at 40-42C) for final 0.5-1 kg if needed. |
| **Weigh-in morning (Saturday)** | Nothing until after weigh-in | — | — | Nothing | Weigh in. Then immediately begin rehydration protocol below. |

**Additional fight week tactics:**
- Low-fiber diet from Wednesday onward to reduce gut content weight (~0.5 kg)
- A hot Epsom salt bath (20 min) the night before weigh-in can shed an extra 0.5-1 kg through sweat
- Sauna use should be limited to 15-20 minute sessions maximum with monitoring — never alone
- Weigh yourself multiple times per day during fight week to track progress

## 9. Rehydration & Refueling Plan (Post-Weigh-In)

Begin immediately after stepping off the scale. The goal is to regain as much of the cut weight as possible before fight time.

| Priority | Protocol | Timing |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Fluid** | 1.5 L per kg of bodyweight lost. Sip steadily — do not chug. | Begin immediately, spread over 2-4 hours |
| **Electrolytes** | Mandatory with every drink. Sodium, potassium, magnesium. Use oral rehydration solution (ORS) or electrolyte mix. | With every fluid intake |
| **Carbohydrates** | 8-10 g/kg over the rehydration period. Simple carbs first (white rice, bananas, sports drinks), then gradually add complexity. | Begin within 30 min of weigh-in |
| **Protein** | 1.5-2 g/kg spread across meals. Lean sources (chicken, whey). | With each meal |
| **Fat** | Keep low initially — fats slow gastric emptying and can cause GI distress. Add moderate fat only in final pre-fight meal. | Minimal for first 4 hours, moderate after |

### Sample Post-Weigh-In Rehydration Schedule

| Time After Weigh-In | Action |
|---------------------|--------|
| 0-30 min | 500 ml ORS + banana + 30 g whey protein shake |
| 30-60 min | Sip 500 ml sports drink. Small bowl of white rice + chicken (200 g rice, 100 g chicken). |
| 1-2 hours | 500 ml water with electrolytes. Rice cakes with honey. |
| 2-4 hours | Full meal: 250 g white rice, 150 g chicken, steamed vegetables, small amount of butter. Continue sipping fluids. |
| 4-6 hours | Continue sipping fluids. Light snack if hungry: banana, yogurt, crackers. |
| Pre-fight meal (3-4 hours before fight) | Familiar, moderate meal: rice + chicken + banana. Nothing new or heavy. Small amount of caffeine if part of routine. |

> **Timing is critical.** You need a minimum of 12-24 hours between weigh-in and fight time to rehydrate properly. Same-day weigh-ins are significantly more dangerous — the rehydration window is too short to fully recover.

## 11. Daily Weigh-In Protocol

Consistent, accurate daily weigh-ins are essential for tracking cut progress and making timely adjustments.

| Parameter | Protocol |
|-----------|----------|
| **Time** | Immediately upon waking |
| **Condition** | Fasted — no food, no water. After using the bathroom. |
| **Clothing** | Underwear only, or naked. Same every day. |
| **Scale** | Same digital scale every day. Place on a hard, flat surface (not carpet). |
| **Recording** | Log weight in a tracking app or spreadsheet immediately. Include the date. |
| **Trend tracking** | Use a 7-day rolling average, not single-day readings. Daily fluctuations of 0.5-1.5 kg are normal (water, food, bowel movements). |

### Decision Rules

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| 7-day average is on target | Continue as planned |
| 7-day average is 0.5+ kg ahead of schedule | Slightly increase carbs on training days (+30 g) to protect performance |
| 7-day average is 0.5+ kg behind schedule | Add 20-30 min low-intensity cardio on 2-3 rest days. Do NOT increase deficit beyond 600 kcal. |
| 7-day average is 1+ kg behind schedule | Add cardio AND reduce rest day carbs by 30 g. Consult with coach/nutritionist. |
| Weight stalls for 10+ days | Likely water retention masking fat loss. Stay the course for 3-4 more days. If no change, reduce carbs by 20 g on rest days. |

## 12. WADA Compliance & Supplement Safety

### Verification Protocol

- **Before taking ANY supplement**, verify its status at [globaldro.com](https://www.globaldro.com) — the Global Drug Reference Online maintained by WADA-accredited agencies.
- Check every time you buy a new batch — even the same brand and product can change formulation between production runs.
- If a substance is not listed on GlobalDRO, do not assume it is permitted. Contact your sanctioning body for guidance.

### Recommended Certifications

| Certification | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------|
| **NSF Certified for Sport** | Independent testing for banned substances and label accuracy. Gold standard. |
| **Informed Sport** | Batch-tested by LGC for prohibited substances. Widely recognised internationally. |
| **BSCG Certified Drug Free** | Tests for 500+ drugs including WADA-prohibited substances. |

> **Use certified brands whenever possible.** The cost difference is minimal compared to the career-ending consequences of a positive test.

### Common Contamination Risks

| Product Category | Risk | Details |
|-----------------|------|---------|
| Pre-workouts | High | May contain undeclared stimulants (DMAA, DMHA, higenamine) that are prohibited in competition |
| Herbal supplements | High | May contain unlisted ingredients, heavy metals, or pro-hormones |
| Imported products | High | Products manufactured outside major regulated markets carry higher contamination risk |
| Fat burners / weight loss products | Very high | Frequently contain prohibited stimulants or diuretics |
| Protein powders (major brands) | Low | Generally safe, but verify certification |
| Creatine monohydrate (major brands) | Very low | One of the safest and most well-studied supplements |

### Athlete Responsibility

If competing under any athletic commission or sanctioning body, the athlete is **personally responsible for everything they ingest**. This is a strict liability, "no excuse" policy:

- "I didn't know it was in there" is not a valid defence.
- "My coach/nutritionist told me to take it" is not a valid defence.
- "The label didn't list it" is not a valid defence.

**You are responsible. Verify everything.**
