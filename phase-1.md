# Phase 1 — Base Camp (Weeks 1-3)

> **Phase:** Accumulation / Base Building | **Duration:** March 16 - April 5, 2026
> **Weight Target:** 89 kg → 85 kg (gradual deficit ~1.3 kg/week)
> **Training Focus:** Movement quality, baseline establishment, progressive overload
> **RPE Progression:** Week 1: 6-7 | Week 2: 7-8 | Week 3: 8-9
> **1RMs:** Squat 150 kg | Deadlift 180 kg | Bench 115 kg | OHP 75 kg

---

## Phase 1 Overview

Phase 1 builds the **aerobic and structural foundation** the entire camp depends on. The goal is not to destroy yourself in Week 1 — it's to establish patterns, dial in technique under moderate load, and begin a sustainable caloric deficit that will carry through all 9 weeks.

Strength work uses **linear progressive overload** across the 3 weeks. Loads start at 65-70% 1RM and climb to 80-85% by Week 3. MMA sessions focus on volume and technique accumulation — sparring is limited to once per week (Thursday) and starts at controlled intensity.

### Phase 1 Goals

- Establish 2x/day training rhythm (AM strength + PM MMA)
- Build aerobic base with 2-3 cardiac output sessions per week
- Progressive overload on all compound lifts
- One hard sparring session per week (Thursday)
- Begin caloric deficit (~500 kcal/day → ~0.5 kg/week from diet alone)
- Baseline conditioning test (Week 0 or early Week 1)
- Establish morning weight tracking habit

### Tempo Key

All strength movements use the following tempo notation unless otherwise noted:
- **3** = 3 sec eccentric (lowering)
- **1** = 1 sec pause at bottom
- **2** = 2 sec concentric (controlled lift)
- **0** = no pause at top

Week 3 shifts to **3-1-X-0** on compound lifts (X = explosive concentric) to prepare for Phase 2.

### Intensity Key

| Marker | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 🟢 | Easy — recovery pace, low CNS demand |
| 🟡 | Moderate — working hard but controlled |
| 🔴 | Hard — high intensity, high CNS demand |
