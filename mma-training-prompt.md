## Program Requirements

### Structure
- 4-week mesocycle: weeks 1-3 progressive overload, week 4 deload/recovery
- Each day fully programmed (no "pick your own" — tell him exactly what to do)
- Include time estimates for each session
- Double sessions on some days are fine (AM strength / PM MMA)
- Minimum 6 hours between AM and PM sessions

### Session Types Needed

**MMA Skills (3-4x/week)**
- Striking technique days (boxing, kickboxing, muay thai drills)
- Grappling days (wrestling, BJJ positional drilling, live rolls)
- Mixed sparring / situational drilling
- Include specific round timers (e.g., 5x5min rounds with 1min rest)
- Drill names should be specific and real (e.g., "jab-cross-hook into level change double leg" not just "combo drills")
- Shadow boxing rounds with specific focus (e.g., "3x3min: round 1 jab-cross only, round 2 add hooks and uppercuts, round 3 add kicks")
- Clinch work, dirty boxing, cage wrestling drills
- Positional BJJ: guard retention, back control, mount escapes — specific positions and reps
- Live sparring with constraints (e.g., "boxing only first 2 rounds, add kicks round 3, full MMA round 4-5")

**Strength & Power (3x/week)**
- Compound lifts: back squat, front squat, conventional deadlift, trap bar deadlift, bench press, overhead press, weighted pull-ups, barbell rows, Romanian deadlift
- Fighter-specific movements: Turkish get-ups, landmine rotations, landmine press, hip escapes with band resistance, neck harness work (flexion, extension, lateral), sledgehammer tire hits, battle rope variations
- Power development: hang cleans, push press, box jumps, broad jumps, med ball rotational throws, med ball slams, kettlebell swings
- Grip strength: farmer carries, dead hangs, fat grip work, gi pull-ups if available, plate pinch holds
- Use RPE and percentage-based loading — provide estimated working weights for an 87-90kg lifter with 3-4 years training (estimate 1RMs: squat ~150kg, deadlift ~180kg, bench ~115kg, OHP ~75kg)
- Anti-rotation and core stability every session: Pallof press, ab wheel, hanging leg raises, Copenhagen planks, dead bugs with band, landmine rotations
- Prehab every session (5-10 min): banded external rotations, face pulls, band pull-aparts, TKE squats, hip 90/90 switches, ankle mobility work
- No bodybuilding isolation work — every exercise must have a direct carryover to fighting

**Energy Systems / Cardio (2-3x/week)**
- Cardiac output (Joel Jamieson method): 30-40min sustained at 130-150 BPM — options: assault bike, rowing, swimming, light jogging. Track with heart rate monitor.
- High Intensity Interval Training: assault bike sprints (30s on/30s off x 8-10), rowing intervals (500m repeats), battle rope Tabata (20s on/10s off x 8), ski erg intervals
- Fight-specific conditioning: circuit training mimicking fight pace — 5min rounds with 1min rest, using bag work, sprawls, burpees, ground-up transitions, shadow boxing
- Alactic power intervals: 8-10 second all-out sprints with full recovery (1:12 work:rest ratio), hill sprints, sled pushes
- Road work: 3-5km tempo runs at conversational pace, 200m hill sprint repeats
- Specify exact heart rate zones for every conditioning piece:
  - Zone 1 (recovery): <130 BPM
  - Zone 2 (cardiac output): 130-150 BPM
  - Zone 3 (threshold): 150-170 BPM
  - Zone 4 (VO2max): 170-185 BPM
  - Zone 5 (alactic): max effort, <10 seconds

### Progressive Overload Plan
- Week 1: Base volume, moderate intensity (RPE 6-7), focus on movement quality and establishing baseline
- Week 2: Increase load 2.5-5% on main lifts OR add 1 set to accessories. Conditioning adds 1 round or 2 minutes. RPE 7-8.
- Week 3: Peak volume and intensity (RPE 8-9). Highest conditioning volume. Most demanding sparring.
- Week 4: Deload — drop volume 40-50% (fewer sets, same exercises), keep intensity at 70-75%, no sparring (light technical rolls only), conditioning is Zone 1-2 only. Focus on mobility, recovery, and technique refinement.
