## Exercise Library

### Compound Barbell Lifts

| Exercise | Primary Muscles | Key Cues | MMA Transfer |
|----------|----------------|----------|--------------|
| Back Squat | Quads, glutes, adductors, erectors | Brace hard, spread the floor, hips and knees break together. Drive up through mid-foot. | Lower-body force for level changes, takedown defense, punching power from the ground up. |
| Front Squat | Quads, glutes, upper back, core | Elbows up the whole way. Sit straight down between hips. Drive up keeping torso vertical. | Upright torso pattern mimics clinch posture and double-leg finishes. Heavy anterior core demand. |
| Deadlift | Posterior chain, lats, grip | Push the floor away, lock lats, bar drags up the legs. Stand tall — squeeze glutes at lockout. | Total-body posterior chain for throws, slams, sprawls, and heavy rear-hand strikes. Grip endurance under load. |
| Romanian Deadlift (RDL) | Hamstrings, glutes, erectors | Push hips straight back. Bar stays on thighs/shins. Feel deep hamstring stretch, then drive hips forward. | Eccentric hamstring strength reduces knee-injury risk from sprawling and kicking. |
| Bench Press | Pecs, anterior delts, triceps | Control bar to lower chest, drive through feet, press bar in slight arc back over shoulders. | Horizontal pressing power for frames, stiff-arms, bench pressing opponent off from bottom. |
| Overhead Press | Deltoids, triceps, upper traps, core | Press bar straight up — move head out of the way, then back through. Lock out over mid-foot. | Overhead strength for posting on cage, hand fighting, resisting being driven down in clinch. |
| Barbell Row | Lats, rhomboids, rear delts, biceps | Pull bar to lower sternum, squeeze shoulder blades at top, control the negative. Torso stays still. | Pulling strength for clinch work, underhook control, arm drags, and guillotine finishes. |
| Hang Clean | Quads, glutes, traps, upper back | Explode hips, shrug, pull elbows high, catch in front rack with quarter squat. | Triple extension = same motor pattern as shooting a double-leg or throwing an uppercut. Rate of force development. |
| Push Press | Deltoids, triceps, quads, core | Quick dip keeping torso vertical, then drive aggressively through legs and press overhead. | Legs-to-arms force transfer — same kinetic chain used in every punch and clinch off-balancing. |

### Single-Leg / Unilateral

| Exercise | Primary Muscles | Key Cues | MMA Transfer |
|----------|----------------|----------|--------------|
| RFESS (Bulgarian Split Squat) | Quads, glutes, adductors | Lower straight down until rear knee nearly touches floor. Drive through front heel. Keep torso upright. | Single-leg strength for kicking, sprawling off one leg, maintaining base when opponent grabs a leg. |
| Single-Leg RDL | Hamstrings, glutes, core (anti-rotation) | Hinge at hip, free leg extends behind as counterbalance. Back stays flat. Squeeze glute to stand. | Standing leg stability for kicks, single-leg TD defense. Anti-rotation mirrors rotational chaos of grappling. |
| Walking Lunges | Quads, glutes, hip flexors | Take a long step forward, lower rear knee toward floor, drive through front heel. Stay tall. | Dynamic single-leg strength for shooting entries, level changes, maintaining balance in the pocket. |
| Step-Ups | Quads, glutes | Drive through top foot to stand on box. Do NOT push off bottom foot. Control descent. | Concentric single-leg power for standing up from bottom, penetration steps for takedowns. |
| Pistol Squat (Progression) | Quads, glutes, hip flexors, ankles | Sit back and down on one leg, keeping heel flat. Use band or box assist as needed. | Ultimate single-leg strength/mobility for recovering balance after sweeps, deep scramble positions. |

### Power / Explosive

| Exercise | Primary Muscles | Key Cues | MMA Transfer |
|----------|----------------|----------|--------------|
| Box Jumps | Quads, glutes, calves | Swing arms, extend hips-knees-ankles, land softly on box with full foot contact. Step down. | Rate of force development for explosive takedown entries, sprawls, and closing distance. |
| Broad Jumps | Glutes, quads, hamstrings | Swing arms forward, drive hips and jump for max distance. Absorb landing with soft knees. | Horizontal explosive power — exact vector for shooting takedowns and closing distance for strikes. |
| Med Ball Overhead Slam | Lats, core, shoulders | Brace core, slam ball into ground as hard as possible using whole body. Hinge and follow through. | Full-body power expression similar to hammerfists from top or slam finishes. |
| Med Ball Rotational Slam | Obliques, core, hips | Rotate hips first, then trunk, then arms — slam ball into wall. Load from back hip. | Directly trains rotational power sequence of hooks, overhands, and round kicks. |
| Med Ball Chest Pass | Pecs, triceps, core | Drive ball off chest explosively into wall. Catch and immediately redirect. Stay on balls of feet. | Horizontal pushing speed for frames, posting, bench-pressing opponent off you. |
| KB Swings | Glutes, hamstrings, core | Snap hips forward — arms just go along for the ride. Squeeze glutes hard at top. | Hip snap transfers to striking power, sprawl speed, and bridging/shrimping. High-rep = posterior chain endurance. |
| KB Snatch | Glutes, hamstrings, shoulders | Hike back, snap hips, guide bell around wrist and punch through to lockout overhead. | Combines explosive hip extension with overhead stability and grip. Full-body power expression. |
| Battle Rope Slams | Shoulders, core, lats, grip | Alternate arms or slam both together. Generate force from hips. Keep a rhythm. | Upper-body power-endurance under fatigue — simulates repeated explosive arm actions of punching in later rounds. |

### Core / Anti-Movement

| Exercise | Primary Muscles | Key Cues | MMA Transfer |
|----------|----------------|----------|--------------|
| Pallof Press | Obliques, transverse abdominis | Press handle straight out from chest and hold. Resist rotation. Keep hips square. | Anti-rotation core stability — resisting being twisted in clinch, scrambles, and when absorbing body shots. |
| Ab Wheel Rollout | Rectus abdominis, obliques, lats | Roll forward slowly, keeping hips extended. Go as far as you can control, then pull back with abs. | Anti-extension strength for maintaining posture in clinch, resisting back bends in wrestling. |
| Hanging Leg Raises | Hip flexors, lower abs, grip | Raise legs to parallel (or toes to bar). Control descent — no swinging. Initiate from core. | Hip flexor strength for guard retention, high kicks, knee strikes. Grip endurance while working core. |
| Copenhagen Plank | Adductors, obliques | Top foot on bench, lift hips and bottom leg to form straight line. Hold. | Adductor and lateral core strength for closed guard, body locks, and groin injury prevention. |
| Dead Bug | Deep core stabilizers | Extend opposite arm and leg toward floor while keeping low back glued to mat. | Core stabilizing spine while limbs move independently — exactly what happens during transitions. |
| Farmer's Carry | Traps, grip, core (anti-lateral flexion) | Walk with short, controlled steps. Stay tall — don't lean. Squeeze handles hard. | Total-body stability, grip endurance, and postural strength. Directly transfers to clinch endurance. |
| Suitcase Carry | Obliques, core, grip | Walk resisting the urge to lean toward loaded side. Stay perfectly upright. | Unilateral anti-lateral flexion — resisting being pulled to one side in clinch, defending arm drags. |
| Turkish Get-Up | Full body | Roll to elbow, post on hand, bridge hips, sweep leg under to kneeling, stand up. Eyes on bell. | Most MMA-specific core exercise. Every phase mimics a grappling transition. |

### Upper Body Accessories

| Exercise | Key Cues | MMA Transfer |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| Weighted Pull-Ups | Initiate with lats, pull chin over bar, control descent to full extension. | Pulling strength for clinch control, guillotines, arm drags. Grip under load. |
| Dumbbell Row | Pull dumbbell to hip, squeezing shoulder blade back and down. | Unilateral pulling for single-collar ties, underhook battles. |
| Landmine Press | Press bar up and forward at slight angle. Squeeze core to prevent rotation. | Angled pressing mimics jab/cross. Anti-rotation demand mirrors posting. |
| Incline DB Press | Press dumbbells up and slightly inward. Lower to shoulders with control. | Upper chest pressing for frames from bottom, posting on cage. |
| Push-Up Variations | Standard, Diamond, Archer, Clap — all with full ROM and proper alignment. | Pressing endurance, explosive frames, short-range pushing power. |
| Dips | Lower until upper arms parallel to floor, press to full lockout. | Pressing from depressed shoulder position — posting on mat, pressing up from bottom. |
| Face Pulls | Pull rope to face, split hands apart and externally rotate. Squeeze for a beat. | Shoulder health and postural balance against high volumes of punching. |
| Band Pull-Aparts | Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades together. Slow return. | Scapular health and posture maintenance. Daily prehab movement. |

### Grip & Neck

| Exercise | Key Cues | MMA Transfer |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| Plate Pinch Holds | Squeeze two plates together (smooth sides out). Hold for time. | Pinch grip for controlling wrists, maintaining grip on sweaty opponent. |
| Towel Pull-Ups | Perform pull-ups while gripping towels draped over bar. | Grip endurance on thick, pliable surface — mimics controlling sweaty wrist/ankle. |
| Fat Grip Rows | Row with thick handles. Reduce weight 20-30% vs normal. | Open-hand grip strength for no-gi grappling and wrist control. |
| Neck Harness (4-Way) | Controlled flexion/extension through comfortable ROM. 3-4 sec per rep. NEVER do neck bridges. | Strong neck directly reduces concussion risk. Resists guillotines, can openers, cranks. |
| Band Neck Isometrics | Hold head neutral against band pull. 10-20 sec holds, all 4 directions. | Low-impact daily neck strengthening. Isometric endurance for real-time resistance. |

### Conditioning Modalities

| Exercise | Key Cues | MMA Transfer |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| Assault Bike Intervals | Push/pull with arms, drive with legs. All-out for work, easy spin for rest. | Whole-body energy system training. Explosive-burst-then-recover pattern of MMA rounds. |
| Rowing — 500m Repeats | Drive with legs first, then hips, then arms. Target consistent splits. | Full posterior chain conditioning. Lactic tolerance for scrambles and late takedown battles. |
| Jump Rope | Small bounces, turn from wrists. Stay relaxed. | Footwork coordination, calf endurance, rhythm — foundation of striking movement. |
| Sled Push / Pull | Push: drive with legs, stay low. Pull: sit back and row hand-over-hand. | Zero eccentric = heavy conditioning without DOMS. Pushing mimics cage driving, pulling mimics dragging. |
| Shadow Boxing | Throw combinations with intent. Move feet. Visualize an opponent. | Most specific conditioning for striking. Technical practice under fatigue. |
| Bag Work | Throw real combinations. Move head and feet between combos. Hit through the bag. | Full-contact striking conditioning. Builds punch endurance and timing under fatigue. |
| Sprawl-to-Burpee | Drop into sprawl (hips to floor), pop back to feet. Reset stance and repeat. | Most MMA-specific bodyweight conditioning. Combines TD defense with explosive recovery. |

### Prehab / Mobility

| Exercise | Key Cues | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Band External Rotations | Rotate forearm outward, elbow glued to ribs. Slow both directions. | Rotator cuff health — non-negotiable for fighters. |
| 90/90 Hip Switches | Rotate both legs to opposite position in one smooth motion. Chest tall. | Hip IR/ER mobility for guard play, sweeps, hip escapes, kicking. |
| Hip CARs | Largest possible circle with knee — slowly, with maximum tension. | Maintaining full hip ROM. Prevents loss from tight guards and heavy squatting. |
| Thoracic Spine Rotations | Side-lying, rotate top arm up opening chest to ceiling. Knees glued together. | T-spine rotation is the engine of punching power and shot defense. |
| Goblet Squat | Sit between hips, elbows push knees out. Chest tall. | Warm-up for squat patterns. Upright position transfers to clinch posture. |
| World's Greatest Stretch | Lunge → rotate inside arm up → straighten front leg for hamstring stretch. | Most efficient dynamic warm-up. Addresses hip flexors, T-spine, hamstrings, groin. |
| Band Dislocates | Keeping arms straight, raise band overhead and behind back in slow arc. | Full shoulder ROM warm-up. Counteracts tight anterior shoulders from punching. |

## Warm-Up Protocols

### Protocol 1: Upper Body Strength (~10 min)

| Order | Exercise | Duration/Reps | Cue |
|-------|----------|---------------|-----|
| 1 | Jumping jacks | 30 sec | Light and rhythmic, get blood flowing |
| 2 | Arm circles (forward + backward) | 15 sec each direction | Full range, gradually increase size |
| 3 | Shoulder CARs | 5 each direction per arm | Slow, controlled, maximal range |
| 4 | Band pull-aparts | 2 x 15 | Squeeze shoulder blades together |
| 5 | Push-up walkouts (inchworms) | 5 reps | Slow walk out to full plank, brief hold |
| 6 | Thoracic rotations (quadruped) | 5 each side | Rotate to open chest to ceiling |
| 7 | Ramp-up set 1 (main lift) | 8 reps @ empty bar | Focus on bar path and setup |
| 8 | Ramp-up set 2 (main lift) | 5 reps @ ~50% working weight | Lock in cues |

## Cool-Down Protocol (Universal — ~15-20 min)

### Phase 1: Light Movement (5 min)

| Exercise | Duration | Cue |
|----------|----------|-----|
| Walking (or easy bike) | 3 min | Slow pace, bring HR down |
| Deep breathing | 2 min | 4-count inhale through nose, 6-count exhale through mouth |

### Phase 2: Static Stretching (5 min — 30 sec holds)

| Stretch | Duration | Cue |
|---------|----------|-----|
| Standing hamstring stretch (each leg) | 30 sec × 2 | Hinge at hips, flat back |
| Kneeling hip flexor stretch (each side) | 30 sec × 2 | Squeeze glute on back leg, push hips forward |
| Standing quad stretch (each leg) | 30 sec × 2 | Pull heel to glute, knees together |
| Doorway chest stretch (each side) | 30 sec × 2 | Arm at 90° on wall, lean through |
| Overhead lat stretch (each side) | 30 sec × 2 | Grab overhead surface, sink hips away |

### Phase 3: Foam Rolling (5-10 min)

| Area | Duration | Cue |
|------|----------|-----|
| Quads / hip flexors | 60-90 sec each leg | Slow rolls, pause on tender spots |
| IT band / outer thigh | 60 sec each leg | Hip to knee, go slow |
| Upper back (thoracic) | 60-90 sec | Arms crossed, roll mid-back |
| Lats | 60 sec each side | Side-lying, armpit to mid-rib |
| Glutes | 60 sec each side | Lacrosse ball optional, cross ankle over knee |
| Calves | 60 sec each leg | Stack legs, point/flex foot |

## Neck Strengthening Protocol

### Pre-Session Neck Warm-Up (Before Striking & Grappling)

| Exercise | Protocol | Cue |
|----------|----------|-----|
| Isometric Hold — Forward | 10 sec × 2 rounds | Palm on forehead, push at ~50% effort. Resist all movement. |
| Isometric Hold — Backward | 10 sec × 2 rounds | Palm on back of head. Keep chin slightly tucked. |
| Isometric Hold — Left | 10 sec × 2 rounds | Left palm on left temple. Keep shoulders level. |
| Isometric Hold — Right | 10 sec × 2 rounds | Right palm on right temple. Match effort to left side. |

**2 rounds through all 4 directions = ~3 min.** Use 50-70% effort — activation, not max effort.

### Progressive Neck Harness Work (Friday Power Sessions)

| Week | Sets × Reps | Load | Notes |
|------|-------------|------|-------|
| 1 | 2 × 15 | 2.5 kg | Learn the movement. Slow, controlled. |
| 2 | 3 × 12 | 5 kg | Increase volume and load. |
| 3 | 3 × 10 | 7.5 kg | Peak load. Drop back if form degrades. |
| 4+ | 2 × 10 | 5 kg | Maintenance. |

**NEVER do neck bridges or rapid rotations under load.** Each rep = 2 sec concentric, 2 sec eccentric.

## Equipment Needed

### Gym Equipment

- Barbell + plates (Olympic standard)
- Squat rack with pull-up bar
- Adjustable bench (flat + incline)
- Dumbbells (range: 10-50 kg)
- Kettlebells (16 kg, 24 kg, 32 kg)
- Cable machine or resistance bands
- Plyo boxes (20", 24", 30")
- Slam balls (15 lb, 20 lb, 30 lb)
- Med balls (10 lb, 14 lb, 20 lb)
- Battle ropes (1.5-2", 40-50 ft)
- Ab wheel
- Pull-up bar + dip station
- Sled + turf/track
- Assault bike and/or rowing ergometer
- Foam roller + lacrosse ball
- Resistance bands (mini + long loop)
- Neck harness
- Fat Gripz (or thick-handle implements)
- PVC pipe or broomstick

### MMA Training Gear

- MMA gloves (4 oz for grappling, 7 oz for sparring)
- Boxing gloves (16 oz for sparring, 12-14 oz for pads/bag)
- Hand wraps (180" minimum)
- Mouthguard (custom-fitted recommended)
- Groin protector / cup
- Headgear (mandatory from Week 3+)
- Shin guards
- Rashguard + fight shorts
- Jump rope (speed rope)
- Heavy bag (70-100 lbs)
- Focus mitts + Thai pads
- Gym towels (for towel pull-ups / grip work)
- Old gi (for gi pull-ups)
- Timer (round timer app or Tabata timer)
