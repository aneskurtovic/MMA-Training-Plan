# Phase 3 — Sharpening (Weeks 7-8)

> **Phase:** Realization / Taper | **Duration:** April 27 - May 10, 2026
> **Weight Target:** 81 kg → 79 kg (within water-cut range of 77 kg)
> **Training Focus:** Technical sharpness, speed work, game plan drilling, volume reduction
> **RPE:** 7-8 (controlled intensity, reduced volume)
> **Key Changes:** Strength 2x/week (down from 3), no hard sparring in Week 8, speed/timing replaces volume

---

## Phase 3 Overview

This phase is about **subtraction, not addition**. You are not building fitness here — you are sharpening what you already have and allowing your body to supercompensate. Every session has a purpose. If it doesn't make you sharper for fight night, cut it.

### Key Principles

- **Strength drops to 2 sessions/week** (Monday and Wednesday only — Friday power session is removed)
- **Loads drop to 70-75% of 1RM** with emphasis on bar speed and explosiveness
- **No hard sparring in Week 8** — light technical sparring only
- **Week 7** — moderate sparring is still permitted (controlled rounds, trusted partners only)
- **Conditioning becomes shorter and sharper** — intervals under 10 seconds, full recovery between sets
- **Game plan specific drilling increases** — positional sparring, situational reps, entries and exits
- **Mental preparation begins** — daily visualization, fight strategy review, routine-setting
- **Water loading protocol discussion** — coordinate with coach and reference weight-management section

## Notes for Coach

1. **Monitor fatigue closely.** If the athlete reports unusual soreness, sleep disruption, or mood changes, cut volume further.
2. **Sparring partners in Week 7** should be briefed — controlled rounds, no ego, game plan focus.
3. **Week 8 has zero hard sparring.** This is non-negotiable. The fight is won in the weeks before, not the week of.
4. **Weight should be trending toward 79-80 kg** naturally by end of Week 8. If still above 81 kg, consult with nutritionist before beginning water cut.
5. **Transition to Phase 4 (Fight Week)** begins Sunday, May 11.
