# Safety & Protocols

---

## Concussion Protocol

### Graduated Return-to-Play (6-Step Protocol)

> **Mandatory minimum stand-down: 7 days from the date of concussion.** No athlete may advance through these steps in less than 7 days total. Each step requires a minimum of 24 hours symptom-free before progressing to the next.

| Step | Activity | Objective | Duration |
|------|----------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Complete physical and cognitive rest | Recovery | 24-48 hours minimum |
| 2 | Light aerobic exercise (walking, stationary bike at <70% max HR) | Increase heart rate | 1 day minimum |
| 3 | Sport-specific exercise (shadow boxing, movement drills — no contact, no head impact risk) | Add movement complexity | 1 day minimum |
| 4 | Non-contact training drills (pad work, grappling drills at 50%, technical sparring with zero contact to head) | Increase intensity, restore coordination | 1 day minimum |
| 5 | Full contact practice (return to normal sparring with trusted partners, coach monitoring) | Restore confidence, test functional recovery | 1 day minimum |
| 6 | Return to competition | Full clearance | Medical sign-off recommended |

- If symptoms return at any step, drop back to the previous step and wait 24 hours before retrying.
- A second concussion within 12 months requires medical clearance before returning to any contact training.

### Red Flags — Go to the Emergency Room Immediately

> **If the athlete or their training partner exhibits ANY of the following after a head impact, call emergency services or go to the ER immediately. Do not wait.**

| Red Flag | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Repeated vomiting | Two or more episodes after head impact |
| Seizure | Any seizure activity, even brief |
| Loss of consciousness >30 seconds | Unresponsive or unable to be woken |
| Worsening headache | Headache that intensifies over time rather than improving |
| Slurred speech | Difficulty forming words or sentences that weren't present before impact |
| Weakness or numbness | One-sided weakness, numbness in extremities, or difficulty with coordination |
| Unequal pupils | One pupil visibly larger than the other |
| Unusual drowsiness | Cannot stay awake, extreme confusion, or combativeness |

## Sparring Safety

### Partner Matching

- Match sparring partners within **+/- 10% bodyweight**. For an 88 kg athlete, acceptable range is 79-97 kg.
- If a significant weight mismatch is unavoidable, the heavier partner must reduce power output proportionally.
- Skill level should be within 1-2 levels. Never pair a first-day beginner against an experienced fighter in live rounds.

### Required Gear by Week

| Timeframe | Required Gear |
|-----------|---------------|
| Week 1-2 (base phase) | 16 oz gloves, mouthguard, cup |
| Week 3+ (intensity phase) | All of the above + headgear mandatory, shin guards mandatory |

> **No exceptions on mouthguard and cup — ever.** Headgear is mandatory from Week 3 onward because sparring intensity increases to 85-90%. If headgear is not available, cap sparring intensity at 60%.

### Corner Person Criteria

Every sparring session must have a designated corner person. This person:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Experience | Must be an experienced fighter or coach who has cornered before |
| Authority | Has the authority to stop rounds at any time, for any reason |
| Monitoring | Actively watches for signs of fatigue, disorientation, or excessive damage |
| Positioning | Stands mat-side with a clear view of both fighters, not distracted |
| Communication | Can communicate clearly with both fighters and the timekeeper |

### Post-Sparring Neuro Check

Perform this check on every athlete after every sparring session, especially after any significant head contact:

| Check | Method | Concern If... |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| Orientation | Ask: "What day is it? Where are we? Who did you spar with?" | Cannot answer correctly or hesitates significantly |
| Balance | Stand on one foot for 10 seconds, eyes closed | Cannot hold balance, sways excessively, falls |
| Recall | Ask about a specific combination or exchange from the round | Cannot recall events from the session |
| Tracking | Follow a finger smoothly left to right, up and down | Eyes jump, cannot track smoothly, reports dizziness |

> **If the athlete fails any part of the neuro check:** no more training that day, monitor for 24 hours, apply the concussion protocol if symptoms persist.

## Injury Modification Table

> **Training through pain is not toughness — it is stupidity that costs you training weeks.** Use these substitutions to keep training volume up while protecting injured areas.

| Body Part | Avoid | Substitutes | See Doctor If... |
|-----------|-------|-------------|------------------|
| **Shoulder** | Overhead pressing, bench press at full ROM, heavy pull-ups, throwing combinations with full extension | Landmine press (neutral path), floor press (limited ROM), band pull-aparts, isometric holds at pain-free angles | Pain at rest, inability to raise arm above shoulder height, clicking/catching with sharp pain, numbness down the arm |
| **Knee** | Deep squats, lunging with full depth, explosive jumping/landing, heavy leg press at full ROM | Box squat to pain-free depth, leg press at partial ROM, step-ups (low box), wall sits, hamstring curls, hip thrusts | Swelling that doesn't resolve in 48 hours, locking/giving way, inability to fully straighten or bend, pain walking downstairs |
| **Lower Back** | Heavy deadlifts, loaded spinal flexion (sit-ups, GHD), back squats with heavy load, explosive rotational movements | Hip hinge with light KB (RDL pattern), bird dogs, dead bugs, McGill Big 3 (curl-up, side plank, bird dog), belt squat | Pain radiating down either leg, numbness/tingling in feet, loss of bladder/bowel control, pain that worsens despite 5+ days rest |
| **Neck** | Neck bridges, heavy shrugs, sparring with head contact, any exercise that loads the neck under flexion | Isometric 4-way holds (light, pain-free), gentle ROM exercises, upper trap stretches, avoid headgear sparring until cleared | Pain radiating into arms/hands, persistent headache associated with neck movement, weakness in grip, dizziness when turning head |
| **Wrist/Hand** | Heavy gripping (deadlift, pull-ups, farmer's carry), wrist extension under load, heavy bag work without wraps | Straps for pulling movements, push-ups on fists or parallettes, hook grip alternatives, open-hand guard work, focus mitt work with proper wrapping | Swelling that doesn't resolve in 48 hours, inability to grip at all, visible deformity, numbness in fingers, pain on the snuffbox (scaphoid fracture risk) |
| **Ankle** | Running, box jumps, any plyometrics, heavy calf work, kicking with the injured side | Cycling or rowing for cardio, seated calf raises (light), balance board work at pain-free ROM, upper body and grappling focus | Inability to bear weight, significant bruising/swelling, pain on both sides of the ankle (possible fracture), instability (ankle gives way) |
| **Ribs** | Trunk rotation under load, heavy bag work, sparring (any contact), exercises requiring bracing under load (squats, deadlifts) | Machine-based isolation work (leg extension, hamstring curl, cable exercises), light technique drilling with no contact, walking/cycling for conditioning | Pain with deep breathing that worsens, bruising that spreads, pain on specific rib when pressed, shortness of breath |

#### General Injury Rules

1. If an exercise causes **sharp pain**, stop immediately. Dull discomfort during warm-up that resolves within 2-3 minutes is generally acceptable.
2. Never increase load on a modified exercise until the original exercise can be performed pain-free.
3. Ice acute injuries for 15-20 minutes, 3-4 times per day for the first 48-72 hours.
4. Log all injuries and modifications in your training journal — patterns reveal root causes.
