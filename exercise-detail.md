# Exercise Guide

Click any exercise name in the training plan to jump here. Each exercise includes setup, execution, common mistakes, and how it transfers to MMA fighting.

---

## Back Squat

**Category:** Compound Barbell Lifts  
**Primary Muscles:** Quads, glutes, adductors, erectors  
**Equipment:** Barbell, squat rack

### Setup

Bar on upper traps (high bar) or rear delts (low bar). Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out.

### Execution

Brace hard, spread the floor, hips and knees break together. Drive up through mid-foot.

### Common Mistakes

- Knees caving
- forward lean / chest drop
- cutting depth short
- losing brace at the bottom.

### MMA Application

Builds the lower-body force production that powers level changes, takedown defense (standing up under pressure), and knockout-level punching from the legs up.

## Front Squat

**Category:** Compound Barbell Lifts  
**Primary Muscles:** Quads, glutes, upper back, core  
**Equipment:** Barbell, squat rack

### Setup

Bar in front rack (clean grip or cross-arm). Elbows high, chest tall. Feet shoulder-width.

### Execution

Elbows up the whole way. Sit straight down between your hips. Drive up keeping torso vertical.

### Common Mistakes

- Elbows dropping (bar rolls forward)
- wrist inflexibility forcing cross-arm when clean grip is better
- shallow depth.

### MMA Application

Upright torso pattern mimics posture needed in the clinch and during double-leg finishes. Heavy anterior core demand transfers to resisting being bent over in grappling.

---

## Deadlift

**Category:** Compound Barbell Lifts  
**Primary Muscles:** Posterior chain (glutes, hamstrings, erectors), lats, grip  
**Equipment:** Barbell

### Setup

Feet hip-width under bar, shins near bar. Hinge at hips, grip just outside knees. Neutral spine, lats engaged.

### Execution

Push the floor away, lock lats, bar drags up the legs. Stand tall — squeeze glutes at lockout.

### Common Mistakes

- Rounding the lower back
- jerking the bar off the floor
- hips shooting up first
- hyperextending at lockout.

### MMA Application

Total-body posterior chain strength for explosive hip extension — the engine behind throws, slams, sprawls, and heavy rear-hand strikes. Grip endurance under load.

---

## Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

**Category:** Compound Barbell Lifts  
**Primary Muscles:** Hamstrings, glutes, erectors  
**Equipment:** Barbell

### Setup

Stand with bar at hip height (from rack or after a deadlift). Feet hip-width, slight knee bend, shoulders packed.

### Execution

Push hips straight back like closing a car door. Bar stays on the thighs/shins. Feel a deep hamstring stretch, then drive hips forward.

### Common Mistakes

- Bending the knees too much (turns into a squat)
- rounding the back
- not hinging far enough.

### MMA Application

Eccentric hamstring strength reduces knee-injury risk from sprawling and kicking. Hip-hinge patterning powers underhooks-to-trips and mat returns.

## Bench Press

**Category:** Compound Barbell Lifts  
**Primary Muscles:** Pecs, anterior delts, triceps  
**Equipment:** Barbell, bench, rack

### Setup

Lie on bench, eyes under bar. Retract and depress shoulder blades. Grip slightly wider than shoulder-width. Feet flat on floor.

### Execution

Unrack, control bar to lower chest, drive through feet, press bar in a slight arc back over shoulders.

### Common Mistakes

- Flaring elbows to 90 degrees (shoulder stress)
- bouncing bar off chest
- losing scapular retraction
- lifting hips.

### MMA Application

Horizontal pressing power for frames, stiff-arms, and bench pressing an opponent off you from bottom position. Tricep strength aids in posting and creating distance.

---

## Overhead Press

**Category:** Compound Barbell Lifts  
**Primary Muscles:** Deltoids, triceps, upper traps, core  
**Equipment:** Barbell, squat rack

### Setup

Bar in front rack at collarbone. Feet hip-width, glutes and abs braced.

### Execution

Press bar straight up — move your head out of the way, then back through once bar passes. Lock out directly over mid-foot.

### Common Mistakes

- Excessive back arch (turns into incline press)
- pressing the bar forward instead of straight up
- not bracing core.

### MMA Application

Overhead strength for posting on the cage, hand fighting from collar ties, and resisting being driven down in the clinch. Core stability under overhead load mirrors scramble demands.

---

## Barbell Row

**Category:** Compound Barbell Lifts  
**Primary Muscles:** Lats, rhomboids, rear delts, biceps, erectors  
**Equipment:** Barbell

### Setup

Hinge to roughly 45-degree torso. Overhand or underhand grip just outside knees. Slight knee bend.

### Execution

Pull bar to lower sternum, squeeze shoulder blades at top, control the negative. Torso stays still.

### Common Mistakes

- Excessive torso movement (rowing with momentum)
- pulling to the belly
- standing too upright.

### MMA Application

Pulling strength for clinch work, underhook control, arm drags, and guillotine finishes. Maintains upper-back posture to resist being broken down.

---

## Hang Clean

**Category:** Compound Barbell Lifts  
**Primary Muscles:** Quads, glutes, traps, upper back, forearms  
**Equipment:** Barbell

### Setup

Start standing with bar at hip height, overhand grip. Hinge slightly to bring bar to just above the knee (hang position).

### Execution

Explode hips, shrug, pull elbows high, catch in front rack with a quarter squat. Stand up.

### Common Mistakes

- Arm-pulling instead of hip-driving
- not getting elbows through fast enough
- catching on wrists instead of delts.

### MMA Application

Triple extension (ankle-knee-hip) is the same motor pattern as shooting a double-leg or throwing an uppercut. Teaches rate of force development — how fast you can express strength.

---

## Push Press

**Category:** Compound Barbell Lifts  
**Primary Muscles:** Deltoids, triceps, quads, glutes, core  
**Equipment:** Barbell, squat rack

### Setup

Bar in front rack at collarbone. Feet hip-width.

### Execution

Quick dip (4-6 inches) keeping torso vertical, then drive aggressively through legs and press bar overhead in one motion. Lock out.

### Common Mistakes

- Dipping forward (weight on toes)
- pressing before legs finish driving
- slow dip killing the stretch-shortening cycle.

### MMA Application

Teaches legs-to-arms force transfer — the same kinetic chain used in every punch and in off-balancing an opponent from the clinch. Develops overhead lockout power for cage work.

## RFESS (Bulgarian Split Squat)

**Category:** Single-Leg / Unilateral  
**Primary Muscles:** Quads, glutes, adductors  
**Equipment:** Dumbbells or barbell, bench

### Setup

Rear foot laces-down on bench behind you. Front foot far enough forward that knee stays over ankle at bottom.

### Execution

Lower straight down until rear knee nearly touches floor. Drive through front heel. Keep torso upright.

### Common Mistakes

- Front foot too close to bench (knee shoots forward)
- torso leaning excessively
- rear foot bearing too much load.

### MMA Application

Single-leg strength and balance for kicking, sprawling off one leg, and maintaining base when an opponent grabs a leg. Reduces bilateral strength imbalances that cause injury.

## Single-Leg RDL

**Category:** Single-Leg / Unilateral  
**Primary Muscles:** Hamstrings, glutes, core (anti-rotation)  
**Equipment:** Dumbbell or kettlebell

### Setup

Stand on one leg, soft knee. Hold weight in opposite hand.

### Execution

Hinge at the hip, free leg extends behind you as a counterbalance. Back stays flat. Squeeze glute to stand.

### Common Mistakes

- Rounding the back
- opening the hip (rotating)
- not hinging deep enough
- rushing the movement.

### MMA Application

Posterior chain strength on one leg directly transfers to kicking (standing leg stability), single-leg takedown defense, and landing after throwing knees. Anti-rotation demand mirrors the rotational chaos of grappling.

## Walking Lunges

**Category:** Single-Leg / Unilateral  
**Primary Muscles:** Quads, glutes, hip flexors  
**Equipment:** Dumbbells, barbell, or bodyweight

### Setup

Stand tall, weights at sides or on back.

### Execution

Take a long step forward, lower rear knee toward floor, drive through front heel to step into the next rep. Stay tall.

### Common Mistakes

- Short steps (knee over toe)
- torso collapsing forward
- pushing off rear foot instead of pulling with front leg.

### MMA Application

Dynamic single-leg strength with a locomotion component — transfers to shooting entries, level changes, and maintaining balance while moving forward in the pocket.

---

## Step-Ups

**Category:** Single-Leg / Unilateral  
**Primary Muscles:** Quads, glutes  
**Equipment:** Box (16-20 inches), dumbbells

### Setup

Place one foot fully on the box. Hold dumbbells at sides.

### Execution

Drive through the top foot to stand on the box. Do NOT push off the bottom foot. Control the descent.

### Common Mistakes

- Using the bottom foot to push off (cheating)
- box too low to challenge the movement
- leaning forward.

### MMA Application

Concentric single-leg power for driving off the mat (standing up from bottom), penetration steps for takedowns, and explosive level changes.

---

## Pistol Squat Progression

**Category:** Single-Leg / Unilateral  
**Primary Muscles:** Quads, glutes, hip flexors, ankles  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight (band or box for assistance)

### Setup

Stand on one leg. Extend the other leg in front of you. Arms forward for counterbalance.

### Execution

Sit back and down on one leg, keeping heel flat. Go as low as mobility allows, then drive up. Use a band or box to assisted versions as needed.

### Common Mistakes

- Knee collapsing inward
- heel lifting
- rounding the lower back at the bottom
- using momentum to stand.

### MMA Application

Ultimate expression of single-leg strength and mobility. Translates to recovering balance after being swept or pushed, deep single-leg positions in scrambles, and demonstrating the mobility fighters need at the hips and ankles.

## Box Jumps

**Category:** Power / Explosive  
**Primary Muscles:** Quads, glutes, calves  
**Equipment:** Plyo box (20-30 inches)

### Setup

Stand facing box, feet shoulder-width, arms back.

### Execution

Swing arms, extend hips-knees-ankles, land softly on box with full foot contact. Stand tall, step down.

### Common Mistakes

- Rebounding off the floor (use a reset between reps)
- landing in a deep squat on the box (means the box is too high)
- jumping down instead of stepping.

### MMA Application

Trains rate of force development and reactive power for explosive takedown entries, sprawls, and closing distance to land strikes.

## Broad Jumps

**Category:** Power / Explosive  
**Primary Muscles:** Glutes, quads, hamstrings, calves  
**Equipment:** Open floor space

### Setup

Feet shoulder-width, arms loaded behind you.

### Execution

Swing arms forward, drive hips and jump forward for max distance. Absorb landing with soft knees, stick it.

### Common Mistakes

- Not using the arms
- landing stiff-legged
- not committing to full hip extension.

### MMA Application

Horizontal explosive power — the exact vector used in shooting for takedowns and closing distance for punches. Teaches aggressive hip extension into a target.

## Med Ball Overhead Slam

**Category:** Power / Explosive  
**Primary Muscles:** Lats, core, shoulders, triceps  
**Equipment:** Slam ball (15-30 lbs)

### Setup

Stand tall, ball overhead with arms extended.

### Execution

Brace core, slam ball into the ground as hard as possible using your whole body. Hinge and follow through. Catch the bounce or pick it up.

### Common Mistakes

- Using only arms (not engaging core/hips)
- not following through
- too light a ball.

### MMA Application

Full-body power expression similar to throwing hammerfists from top position or slamming an opponent in a takedown finish. Teaches aggressive downward force production.

## Med Ball Rotational Slam

**Category:** Power / Explosive  
**Primary Muscles:** Obliques, core, hips, shoulders  
**Equipment:** Slam ball (10-20 lbs), wall

### Setup

Stand perpendicular to wall, ball at hip opposite the wall. Feet slightly wider than shoulder-width.

### Execution

Rotate hips first, then trunk, then arms — slam ball into wall. Catch and reset. Load from the back hip.

### Common Mistakes

- Arm-throwing instead of hip-rotating
- feet staying flat (rotate on the ball of the back foot)
- standing too close to the wall.

### MMA Application

Directly trains the rotational power sequence of hooks, overhands, and round kicks. Hip-first rotation is the key to fight-ending power in strikes.

## Med Ball Chest Pass

**Category:** Power / Explosive  
**Primary Muscles:** Pecs, triceps, anterior delts, core  
**Equipment:** Med ball (10-20 lbs), wall or partner

### Setup

Stand facing wall, ball at chest, elbows in.

### Execution

Drive ball off chest explosively into wall, extending arms fully. Catch and immediately redirect. Stay on the balls of your feet.

### Common Mistakes

- Stepping forward (losing base)
- lobbing instead of driving
- slow catch-and-redirect.

### MMA Application

Trains horizontal pushing speed for frames, posting, and bench-pressing an opponent off you. Reactive catch-and-redirect mirrors the speed demands of hand fighting.

## KB Swings

**Category:** Power / Explosive  
**Primary Muscles:** Glutes, hamstrings, core, lats  
**Equipment:** Kettlebell

### Setup

Feet slightly wider than shoulder-width. KB on floor a foot in front of you. Hike the bell back between legs.

### Execution

Snap hips forward — the arms just go along for the ride. Bell should float to chest/eye height (Russian) or overhead (American). Squeeze glutes hard at the top.

### Common Mistakes

- Squatting the swing (should be a hinge)
- using the arms to lift
- hyperextending the back at the top.

### MMA Application

Powerful hip snap transfers directly to striking power, sprawl speed, and bridging/shrimping in grappling. High-rep swings also build posterior chain endurance for later rounds.

---

## KB Snatch

**Category:** Power / Explosive  
**Primary Muscles:** Glutes, hamstrings, shoulders, traps, core  
**Equipment:** Kettlebell

### Setup

Similar to swing start. One hand on the bell.

### Execution

Hike back, snap hips, as the bell rises guide it around your wrist and punch through to lockout overhead. Control the descent by redirecting the bell back between your legs.

### Common Mistakes

- Bell banging the wrist (path is wrong)
- pressing out the top instead of punching through
- not using the hips.

### MMA Application

Combines explosive hip extension with overhead stability and grip — mirrors the full-body power expression of throwing an overhand right or finishing a high-crotch lift. Excellent conditioning effect.

## Battle Rope Slams

**Category:** Power / Explosive  
**Primary Muscles:** Shoulders, core, lats, grip  
**Equipment:** Battle ropes (1.5-2 inch, 40-50 ft)

### Setup

Stand in athletic stance, slight hinge, one rope end in each hand. Anchor point ahead.

### Execution

Alternate arms (waves) or slam both together. Generate force from the hips, not just the shoulders. Keep a rhythm — don't let the ropes go slack.

### Common Mistakes

- Standing too upright (no hip involvement)
- death-gripping (forearms burn out instantly)
- standing too close to the anchor (no tension).

### MMA Application

Upper-body power-endurance under fatigue — simulates the repeated explosive arm actions of punching in later rounds. Bilateral slams train ground-and-pound power; alternating waves build the shoulder endurance needed for long clinch exchanges.

## Weighted Pull-Ups

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Lats, biceps, rear delts, grip  
**Equipment:** Pull-up bar, dip belt or dumbbell between feet

### Setup

Hang from bar, overhand grip slightly wider than shoulders. Add load via belt or dumbbell.

### Execution

Initiate with lats (think elbows to hips), pull chin over bar, control the descent to full extension.

### Common Mistakes

- Kipping / swinging
- not reaching full extension at the bottom
- chin not clearing the bar.

### MMA Application

Pulling strength is essential for clinch control, guillotines, front headlocks, arm drags, and dragging an opponent down from the cage. Grip under load builds lasting clinch endurance.

## Dumbbell Row

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Lats, rhomboids, rear delts, biceps  
**Equipment:** Dumbbell, bench

### Setup

One knee and hand on bench, opposite foot on floor. Flat back, dumbbell hanging at arm's length.

### Execution

Pull dumbbell to hip, squeezing shoulder blade back and down. Lower under control.

### Common Mistakes

- Rotating the torso to heave the weight
- rowing to the shoulder instead of the hip
- rushing reps.

### MMA Application

Unilateral pulling strength for single-collar ties, underhook battles, and controlling an arm in guard. Addresses left-right imbalances common in fighters with a dominant stance.

## Landmine Press

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Chest, shoulders, triceps, core (anti-rotation)  
**Equipment:** Barbell, landmine attachment (or corner)

### Setup

Stand in split stance, hold the end of the barbell at shoulder height with one hand.

### Execution

Press the bar up and forward at a slight angle. Squeeze the core to prevent rotation. Control the return.

### Common Mistakes

- Flaring the elbow
- rotating the torso
- not bracing the core
- pressing straight up instead of along the bar's arc.

### MMA Application

The angled pressing path closely mimics a jab and cross. Single-arm execution demands anti-rotation — the same core stability needed when posting off an opponent or framing from bottom.

---

## Incline DB Press

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Upper pecs, anterior delts, triceps  
**Equipment:** Dumbbells, adjustable bench (30-45 degrees)

### Setup

Sit on incline bench, dumbbells at shoulders, feet flat on floor, back pressed into pad.

### Execution

Press dumbbells up and slightly inward, without clanking them at the top. Lower to shoulders with control.

### Common Mistakes

- Over-arching the back (reducing the incline angle)
- flaring elbows
- bouncing at the bottom.

### MMA Application

Upper chest and shoulder pressing strength for frames from bottom, posting on the cage, and pushing an opponent away. Dumbbell path allows shoulder-friendly range of motion for fighters with existing shoulder wear.

## Push-Up — Standard

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Pecs, triceps, anterior delts, core  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight

### Setup

Hands shoulder-width, body in a straight line from head to heels.

### Execution

Lower chest to the floor, elbows at 45 degrees, press back up. Lock out at top.

### Common Mistakes

- Sagging hips
- flaring elbows to 90 degrees
- partial range of motion.

### MMA Application

Foundational pressing endurance for maintaining frames, escaping bottom positions, and posting. High-rep capacity means pressing strength that lasts into championship rounds.

---

## Push-Up — Diamond

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Triceps, inner chest, core  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight

### Setup

Hands together under chest forming a diamond shape with index fingers and thumbs.

### Execution

Keep elbows tight to the body as you lower. Touch chest to hands, press up.

### Common Mistakes

- Flaring elbows
- letting hips sag
- reducing range of motion.

### MMA Application

Tricep-dominant pressing for short-range pushing — creating frames inside the guard and posting off an opponent's head in scrambles.

---

## Push-Up — Archer

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Chest, triceps, core  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight

### Setup

Wide hand placement. One arm bends while the other stays extended to the side.

### Execution

Lower toward one hand, keeping the other arm straight as a guide. Push back up. Alternate sides.

### Common Mistakes

- Rotating the torso
- bending the straight arm
- shortening the range.

### MMA Application

Asymmetric pressing strength for single-arm frames and posts — common in scrambles where only one arm is available for base. Bridges the gap toward one-arm push-up strength.

---

## Push-Up — Clap

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Pecs, triceps, fast-twitch fibers  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight

### Setup

Standard push-up position.

### Execution

Explode off the floor, clap, land with soft elbows, immediately descend into the next rep.

### Common Mistakes

- Landing with locked elbows (injury risk)
- losing body alignment
- not achieving full flight.

### MMA Application

Upper-body plyometric power for explosive frames and the initial burst of a punch. Trains the stretch-shortening cycle in horizontal pressing — speed kills in striking.

## Dips

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Triceps, lower chest, anterior delts  
**Equipment:** Parallel bars or rings

### Setup

Grip the bars, arms locked, slight forward lean.

### Execution

Lower until upper arms are parallel to floor, press back up to full lockout. Keep elbows tracking over wrists.

### Common Mistakes

- Going too deep (shoulder stress)
- excessive forward lean
- partial range of motion.

### MMA Application

Pressing strength from a depressed shoulder position — directly transfers to posting on the mat, pressing up from bottom, and generating pushing force when pinned against the cage.

## Face Pulls

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Rear delts, external rotators, mid traps  
**Equipment:** Cable machine or band

### Setup

Rope attachment at face height. Step back to create tension.

### Execution

Pull rope to face, splitting hands apart and externally rotating at the end. Squeeze for a beat. Slow return.

### Common Mistakes

- Pulling too low (becomes a row)
- using body momentum
- not externally rotating at the end position.

### MMA Application

Shoulder health and postural balance. Fighters do enormous volumes of pressing and internal rotation (punching, pushing); face pulls counterbalance this to keep shoulders healthy and maintain posture against collar ties.

## Band Pull-Aparts

**Category:** Upper Body Accessories  
**Primary Muscles:** Rear delts, rhomboids, mid traps  
**Equipment:** Resistance band

### Setup

Hold band at arm's length in front of chest, hands shoulder-width, palms down.

### Execution

Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades together until band touches chest. Slow return.

### Common Mistakes

- Shrugging shoulders
- bending elbows
- using momentum.

### MMA Application

Scapular health and posture maintenance. Keeps the upper back strong and shoulders balanced against the high volume of punching and reaching in training. Ideal daily-dose prehab movement.

## Pallof Press

**Category:** Core / Anti-Movement  
**Primary Muscles:** Obliques, transverse abdominis, hip stabilizers  
**Equipment:** Cable machine or band

### Setup

Stand perpendicular to cable, handle at chest. Feet shoulder-width, slight athletic bend.

### Execution

Press handle straight out from chest and hold. Resist the rotation. Return to chest. Keep hips square.

### Common Mistakes

- Letting the cable rotate you
- locking knees
- pressing at an angle instead of straight out.

### MMA Application

Anti-rotation core stability — the ability to resist being twisted or turned in the clinch, during scrambles, and when absorbing body shots. Directly trains the core's primary fight function: resisting unwanted movement.

---

## Ab Wheel Rollout

**Category:** Core / Anti-Movement  
**Primary Muscles:** Rectus abdominis, obliques, lats, hip flexors  
**Equipment:** Ab wheel

### Setup

Kneel on a pad, grip the wheel, arms extended.

### Execution

Roll forward slowly, keeping hips extended (don't sit back). Go as far as you can control, then pull back using your abs.

### Common Mistakes

- Sagging the lower back
- bending at the hips instead of extending
- going farther than you can control.

### MMA Application

Anti-extension core strength for maintaining posture in the clinch, resisting back bends in wrestling, and stabilizing the spine during strikes. The eccentric challenge mirrors being pulled forward by an opponent.

---

## Hanging Leg Raises

**Category:** Core / Anti-Movement  
**Primary Muscles:** Hip flexors, lower abs, grip  
**Equipment:** Pull-up bar

### Setup

Hang from bar with full grip, shoulders packed.

### Execution

Raise legs to parallel (or toes to bar for advanced). Control the descent — no swinging. Initiate from the core, not momentum.

### Common Mistakes

- Swinging
- using momentum
- only lifting knees (if full leg raises are the target)
- losing grip.

### MMA Application

Hip flexor strength for guard retention, high kicks, and knee strikes. Grip endurance while performing a core movement mirrors the simultaneous demands of hanging on an opponent while working your core in the clinch.

---

## Copenhagen Plank

**Category:** Core / Anti-Movement  
**Primary Muscles:** Adductors, obliques, core  
**Equipment:** Bench

### Setup

Side-lying, top foot on bench, bottom foot hanging. Support on forearm.

### Execution

Lift hips and bottom leg so the body forms a straight line. Hold. The top leg's adductor does the heavy lifting.

### Common Mistakes

- Rotating hips forward or back
- sagging at the hip
- holding breath.

### MMA Application

Adductor and lateral core strength critical for closed guard, triangle setups, body lock squeezes, and injury prevention during lateral movement and kicking. Groin injuries are common in MMA — this is direct armor.

---

## Dead Bug

**Category:** Core / Anti-Movement  
**Primary Muscles:** Deep core stabilizers, hip flexors  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight (mat)

### Setup

Lie on back, arms reaching to ceiling, knees bent at 90 degrees over hips. Press low back into floor.

### Execution

Extend opposite arm and leg toward the floor while keeping the low back glued to the mat. Return and switch sides.

### Common Mistakes

- Low back arching off the floor
- moving too fast
- not exhaling fully on extension.

### MMA Application

Teaches the core to stabilize the spine while the limbs move independently — exactly what happens when striking, grappling, and transitioning between positions. Foundation for all other core work.

---

## Bird Dog

**Category:** Core / Anti-Movement  
**Primary Muscles:** Erectors, glutes, deep core stabilizers  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight (mat)

### Setup

Hands-and-knees position, wrists under shoulders, knees under hips.

### Execution

Extend opposite arm and leg until parallel with the floor. Pause. Return. Keep hips level — no rotation.

### Common Mistakes

- Rotating the hips
- arching the back
- rushing through reps
- lifting the limbs too high.

### MMA Application

Anti-rotation and anti-extension in a quadruped position — directly mimics the demands of maintaining base in turtle position and during scrambles on the mat. Excellent warm-up and prehab movement.

## Farmer's Carry

**Category:** Core / Anti-Movement  
**Primary Muscles:** Traps, grip, core (anti-lateral flexion), whole body  
**Equipment:** Heavy dumbbells or farmer's walk handles

### Setup

Stand tall with heavy weights at your sides. Shoulders packed, chest up.

### Execution

Walk with short, controlled steps. Stay tall — don't lean. Squeeze the handles hard. Breathe.

### Common Mistakes

- Leaning to one side
- taking long strides
- shrugging the shoulders
- letting posture break down.

### MMA Application

Total-body stability, grip endurance, and postural strength under load. Directly transfers to clinch endurance, maintaining posture while gripping an opponent, and the general full-body tension required in grappling.

## Suitcase Carry

**Category:** Core / Anti-Movement  
**Primary Muscles:** Obliques, core (anti-lateral flexion), grip  
**Equipment:** Heavy dumbbell or kettlebell

### Setup

Hold a heavy weight in one hand at your side. Stand tall, don't lean toward or away from the load.

### Execution

Walk with controlled steps, resisting the urge to lean toward the loaded side. Stay perfectly upright.

### Common Mistakes

- Leaning toward the weight
- compensating with the opposite hip
- shrugging the loaded shoulder.

### MMA Application

Unilateral anti-lateral flexion — the ability to resist being pulled to one side. Transfers to single-collar tie defense, resisting an arm drag, and maintaining posture when an opponent pulls one side of your body.

## Turkish Get-Up

**Category:** Core / Anti-Movement  
**Primary Muscles:** Shoulders, core, hips, stabilizers (full body)  
**Equipment:** Kettlebell

### Setup

Lie on back, press kettlebell up with one arm. Opposite arm and leg angled out for base.

### Execution

Roll to elbow, post on hand, bridge hips, sweep leg under to kneeling, stand up. Reverse the sequence. Eyes on the bell at all times.

### Common Mistakes

- Rushing the movement
- losing visual contact with the bell
- skipping the hip bridge
- pressing the bell instead of keeping the arm vertical.

### MMA Application

The most MMA-specific core exercise. Every phase mimics a grappling transition: bridging from bottom, posting up to an elbow, creating a frame, building base, and standing from the mat — all while stabilizing load overhead (simulating controlling an opponent's limb).

## Band External Rotations

**Category:** Prehab / Mobility  
**Primary Muscles:** Infraspinatus, teres minor (rotator cuff)  
**Equipment:** Light resistance band

### Setup

Stand with elbow pinned to side at 90 degrees, band attached at elbow height.

### Execution

Rotate forearm outward against band, keeping elbow glued to your ribs. Slow and controlled both directions.

### Common Mistakes

- Elbow drifting away from the body
- using too heavy a band
- moving too fast.

### MMA Application

Rotator cuff health is non-negotiable for fighters. The shoulder absorbs enormous stress from punching, posting, and being manipulated in grappling. This keeps the external rotators strong enough to balance the dominant internal rotators.

## 90/90 Hip Switches

**Category:** Prehab / Mobility  
**Primary Muscles:** Hip internal and external rotators, glutes  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight (mat)

### Setup

Sit on floor with one leg in front (shin perpendicular, externally rotated) and one behind (shin perpendicular, internally rotated). Both knees at 90 degrees.

### Execution

Rotate both legs to the opposite position in one smooth motion. Keep chest tall. Pause at each end position.

### Common Mistakes

- Leaning back
- using hands excessively
- rushing through transitions
- knees not staying at 90 degrees.

### MMA Application

Hip mobility in both internal and external rotation — essential for guard play, sweeps, hip escapes, and kicking. Fighters with restricted hips compensate with their spine, leading to back injuries.

## Hip CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)

**Category:** Prehab / Mobility  
**Primary Muscles:** Hip joint (all surrounding musculature)  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight

### Setup

On hands and knees (or standing). Lift one knee to the side.

### Execution

Make the largest possible circle with your knee — up, out, back, and around — using only the hip joint. Move slowly and with maximum tension. Reverse direction.

### Common Mistakes

- Using momentum
- moving the spine to fake hip range
- rushing
- not engaging max tension.

### MMA Application

Maintaining full usable hip range of motion. Fighters lose hip mobility over time from tight guards, repeated impact, and heavy squatting. CARs preserve the range needed for high kicks, rubber guard, and deep half guard.

## Thoracic Spine Rotations (Open Books)

**Category:** Prehab / Mobility  
**Primary Muscles:** Thoracic spine rotators, obliques  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight (mat)

### Setup

Lie on your side, knees stacked and bent at 90 degrees. Arms extended in front, palms together.

### Execution

Rotate the top arm up and over, opening the chest to the ceiling. Follow the hand with your eyes. Let the thoracic spine rotate — keep knees glued together.

### Common Mistakes

- Rotating from the lumbar spine (knees separate)
- not fully opening
- rushing.

### MMA Application

Thoracic rotation is the engine of punching power and shot defense. Fighters who lose T-spine rotation compensate with the lower back and shoulders, leading to chronic pain. This preserves the rotation needed for hooks, elbows, and looking over the shoulder to defend takedowns.

## Neck Harness (4-Way)

**Category:** Grip / Neck  
**Primary Muscles:** Sternocleidomastoid, upper traps, neck extensors, lateral flexors  
**Equipment:** Neck harness, weight plate or loading pin

### Setup

Attach weight to harness. Perform flexion (chin to chest), extension (look up), and lateral flexion (ear to shoulder) from a seated or standing hinged position.

### Execution

Controlled range of motion through the full movement. 2-second hold at end range. Start very light (5-10 lbs) and add load slowly over weeks.

### Common Mistakes

- Going too heavy too soon
- jerking the weight
- hyperextending at end range
- skipping warm-up sets.

### MMA Application

A strong neck directly reduces concussion risk by limiting rapid head acceleration from strikes. Neck strength also resists guillotines, can openers, and cranks. This is the most injury-preventive exercise a fighter can do.

## Rowing — 500m Repeats

**Category:** Conditioning Modalities  
**Primary Muscles:** Legs, back, arms, cardiovascular system  
**Equipment:** Rowing ergometer (Concept2)

### Setup

Strap feet in, set damper to 4-6. Start with arms extended, shins vertical.

Drive with legs first, then open the hips, then pull handle to sternum. Reverse the order on the recovery. Target consistent split times across repeats.

Full posterior chain conditioning with a pulling emphasis — balances the push-dominant demands of striking. 500m repeats at race pace build the lactic tolerance needed for scramble-heavy rounds and late takedown battles.

## Shadow Boxing

**Category:** Conditioning Modalities  
**Primary Muscles:** Shoulders, core, legs, cardiovascular system  
**Equipment:** None (gloves optional)

### Setup

Fight stance, hands up, chin tucked, weight on balls of feet.

### Execution

Throw combinations with intent. Move your feet between combos. Visualize an opponent — don't just wave your hands. Work defense between offense.

- flat-footed movement
- no defensive movement between combos
- punching with no purpose.

### MMA Application

The most specific conditioning modality for striking. Allows technical practice under fatigue. When used in training programs, shadow boxing rounds serve as active recovery while reinforcing technique and ring generalship.

## Bag Work

**Category:** Conditioning Modalities  
**Primary Muscles:** Shoulders, core, legs, arms, cardiovascular system  
**Equipment:** Heavy bag (70-100 lbs), bag gloves or MMA gloves, hand wraps

### Setup

Wrap hands, glove up. Set up at punching range from the bag. Fight stance.

### Execution

Throw real combinations — jab-cross-hook, not single shots. Move your head and feet between combos. Hit through the bag, not at it.

### Common Mistakes

- Standing flat-footed and winging punches
- not moving between combos
- hitting with bad wrist alignment
- going 100% every round without variation.

### MMA Application

Full-contact striking conditioning that builds punch endurance, timing, and the ability to maintain technique under fatigue. Programmed in 3-5 minute rounds to match fight timing. The bag's feedback teaches fighters to commit to their shots.

## Sprawl-to-Burpee

**Category:** Conditioning Modalities  
**Primary Muscles:** Full body (hip flexors, quads, chest, shoulders, cardiovascular system)  
**Equipment:** Bodyweight

### Setup

Stand in fight stance.

### Execution

Drop into a sprawl (hips to floor, legs back, chest up), immediately pop back to feet, then jump (or skip the jump for speed). Reset stance and repeat.

### Common Mistakes

- Lazy sprawls (hips stay high — defeats the purpose)
- not returning to a fight stance
- pacing too slow for conditioning effect.

### MMA Application

The most MMA-specific bodyweight conditioning movement. Combines takedown defense (the sprawl) with explosive recovery to standing. High-rep sets build the anaerobic endurance to stuff takedowns in the championship rounds when most fights are decided.
