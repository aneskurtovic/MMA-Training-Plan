# Conditioning & Fitness Testing Protocol

## Week 0 — Baseline Testing Protocol

### When to Test

- Schedule testing **2–3 days before Week 1 begins** (e.g., if Week 1 starts Monday, test on the prior Thursday or Friday).
- Avoid testing the day before training starts — allow at least one full rest day between testing and Week 1 Day 1.
- If splitting across two days, do cardiovascular + muscular endurance on Day 1, strength + MMA-specific on Day 2.
- Record the exact date, time of day, and conditions (fasted/fed, sleep quality, etc.) so you can replicate them at re-test.

### Warm-Up Protocol

Perform this warm-up before each testing session (approximately 10 minutes):

1. **5 min easy cardio** — row, bike, or jog at conversational pace
2. **Joint circles** — ankles, knees, hips, shoulders, wrists (10 each direction)
3. **Dynamic stretches** — leg swings (10/side), hip circles (10/side), arm circles (10/side), inchworms (5)
4. **Movement prep** — 5 bodyweight squats, 5 push-ups, 5 pull-apart band pulls

For strength tests, add 2–3 progressive warm-up sets before working sets (e.g., empty bar → 50% → 70% → 85% → 3RM attempt).

### Order of Tests

Run tests from **least fatiguing to most fatiguing** to avoid one test skewing the next. The recommended order below groups tests logically and minimizes interference.

**Rest between tests:** 3–5 minutes between tests within a category, 5–10 minutes between categories.

### Test Battery

#### Cardiovascular

| # | Test | Protocol | What It Measures | Target / Benchmark |
|---|------|----------|------------------|--------------------|
| 1 | Resting Heart Rate | Measure on waking (before standing), 3 consecutive mornings, record average | Aerobic base / cardiac efficiency | < 60 bpm (well-trained); < 50 bpm (elite) |
| 2 | 2 km Row or Run | Time trial — all-out effort. Record finish time, HR at finish, and HR 1 min post-finish | Aerobic capacity (VO2max proxy) | Row: < 7:30 (good), < 7:00 (excellent). Run: < 8:30 (good), < 8:00 (excellent) |
| 3 | 5-min Assault Bike | Max calories in 5 minutes. Record total cals and average HR | Anaerobic capacity + sustained power | > 75 cal (good), > 90 cal (excellent) |

#### Strength

| # | Test | Protocol | What It Measures | Target / Benchmark |
|---|------|----------|------------------|--------------------|
| 4 | Est. 1RM Back Squat | Warm up progressively, work up to a heavy 3RM. Calculate 1RM using Epley formula | Lower body maximal strength | 1.5× bodyweight (good), 2× (excellent) |
| 5 | Est. 1RM Bench Press | Warm up progressively, work up to a heavy 3RM. Calculate 1RM using Epley formula | Upper body pressing strength | 1× bodyweight (good), 1.25× (excellent) |
| 6 | Est. 1RM Deadlift | Warm up progressively, work up to a heavy 3RM. Calculate 1RM using Epley formula | Posterior chain maximal strength | 2× bodyweight (good), 2.5× (excellent) |
| 7 | Max Strict Pull-Ups | Dead hang start, chin over bar, full extension at bottom. No kipping or swinging. Count total reps until failure | Upper body pulling strength-endurance | 10+ (good), 15+ (excellent), 20+ (elite) |

| # | Test | Protocol | What It Measures | Target / Benchmark |
|---|------|----------|------------------|--------------------|

| # | Test | Protocol | What It Measures | Target / Benchmark |
|---|------|----------|------------------|--------------------|

## Monitoring: HRV & Overtraining Indicators

### Morning HRV Protocol

Heart Rate Variability (HRV) is one of the most reliable non-invasive markers of autonomic nervous system readiness. Higher HRV generally indicates better recovery; declining HRV indicates accumulated stress.

| Parameter | Protocol |
|-----------|----------|
| Timing | Measure immediately on waking, before getting out of bed |
| Position | Lying down (supine), relaxed, eyes closed |
| Duration | 60-second reading minimum |
| Equipment | Chest strap (preferred for accuracy) or validated app with phone camera/wrist sensor |
| Recommended apps | Elite HRV, HRV4Training |
| Consistency | Same position, same time, same device every day. Variability in protocol creates noise in the data. |

### Interpreting HRV Trends

Track the **7-day rolling average**, not individual daily readings. Single-day dips are normal and can be caused by alcohol, poor sleep, a hard session the day before, or even meal timing.

| Trend | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| 7-day avg stable or gradually rising | Good recovery, positive adaptation | Continue as planned |
| Single-day dip (returns next day) | Normal variation | No action needed |
| 3+ day downward trend | Functional overreaching — accumulated fatigue is outpacing recovery | Reduce training volume by 20%, prioritise sleep, check nutrition |
| 7-day avg drops **>15% below personal baseline** | Non-functional overreaching or early overtraining | **Immediate deload.** Move to Week 4 protocol regardless of current week. |
| Consistently suppressed for 2+ weeks despite deload | Possible overtraining syndrome | See a sports medicine professional. Training may need to stop entirely for 1-4 weeks. |

### Overtraining Red Flags Checklist

If you notice **3 or more** of the following simultaneously, you are likely overreaching and need to adjust:

- [ ] Persistent fatigue despite adequate sleep (8+ hours)
- [ ] Resting heart rate elevated 5+ consecutive days above baseline
- [ ] Mood disturbances: irritability, apathy, loss of motivation to train
- [ ] Loss of appetite or unexplained weight loss
- [ ] Recurring illness (colds, infections) more than once per month
- [ ] Performance decline despite consistent training and recovery
- [ ] Difficulty sleeping despite fatigue (wired-but-tired feeling)
- [ ] Increased perceived effort on previously manageable loads (RPE 8 on what used to be RPE 6)

> **Overtraining is not a badge of honour — it is a programming failure.** If you reach this state, the solution is rest and recovery adjustment, not more training. Catching overreaching early (via HRV trends and the checklist above) prevents it from becoming overtraining syndrome, which can take months to recover from.

## Week 5 — Re-Testing Protocol

### Scheduling

- Re-test on the **Saturday of Week 4** (the deload week).
- By this point you will have had 3–4 days of reduced training volume, so you should be fresh enough for maximal effort.
- Use the **exact same conditions** as Week 0: same time of day, same equipment, same warm-up, same test order, fasted or fed the same way.

### Procedure

1. Perform the same warm-up protocol as Week 0.
2. Run all 15 tests in the same order.
3. Record results in the "Week 5 Re-Test" column of the recording template above.
4. Calculate the "Change" column (absolute and percentage).

### 1RM Calculation — Epley Formula

Use the same formula for both Week 0 and Week 5 to ensure apples-to-apples comparison:

```
Estimated 1RM = Weight × (1 + Reps ÷ 30)
```

**Examples:**
- 3RM of 100 kg → 1RM = 100 × (1 + 3/30) = 100 × 1.10 = **110 kg**
- 3RM of 80 kg → 1RM = 80 × (1 + 3/30) = 80 × 1.10 = **88 kg**

Note: The Epley formula is most accurate for rep ranges of 1–10. Always use a 3RM for consistency.

| Category | Expected Change | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|

| Squat / Bench / Deadlift 1RM | +3–8% | Neuromuscular adaptations dominate in early weeks |

| Push-Ups in 2 min | +5 to +10 reps | Muscular endurance adapts quickly |

| Bodyweight | −1 to −3 kg (if in deficit) or stable | Depends on nutrition targets |

### Interpreting Results

- **Significant improvement (> 10%):** The program is working well for this quality. Maintain or progress.
- **Modest improvement (3–10%):** Normal for 4 weeks. Keep training consistently.
- **No change or regression:** Check recovery, nutrition, sleep, and stress. Consider whether this area needs more volume or different stimulus in the next block.
- **Strength up + endurance down (or vice versa):** May indicate interference effect — review concurrent training balance.

### Next Steps After Re-Test

- Use Week 5 results as the new baseline for the next 4-week block.
- Adjust training loads: new 3RM estimates should inform working weights.
- Identify weakest areas and consider prioritizing them in the next mesocycle.
- If re-test was missed, schedule it for the first rest day of the next block before loading resumes.
